# Before/After Examples

## Example 1: Em-dash + "not X, it's Y"

**Before:**
> "Watt isn't a data company — it's a signal company. The difference isn't the size of the index, it's what your agent can do with it."

**After:**
> "Watt is a signal company. Your agent reasons across 145,000 signals on people and 55,000 on businesses, in plain language."

**Changes:** Killed two em-dashes and two "not X, it's Y" contrasts. Stated the positioning directly and added a specific number.

---

## Example 2: False agency + jargon

**Before:**
> "In today's competitive landscape, the data tells us that leveraging behavioral signals is a game-changer for teams looking to navigate uncertainty."

**After:**
> "Your agent reads behavioral signals and picks the audience Meta can't see. That's the edge."

**Changes:** Cut the jargon stack (landscape, leverage, game-changer, navigate), removed false agency ("the data tells us"), named the actor and the specific payoff.

---

## Example 3: Throat-clearing + vague declarative

**Before:**
> "Here's the thing: the implications are significant. Most teams genuinely don't realize how structurally broken their data really is."

**After:**
> "Half your data is wrong. You just don't know which half."

**Changes:** Cut the opener and the vague declarative, killed three adverbs (genuinely, really, structurally), replaced abstraction with Watt's actual line.

---

## Example 4: Negative listing + passive

**Before:**
> "It wasn't a pivot. It wasn't a rebrand. A new category was created."

**After:**
> "We created a category: signal engineering."

**Changes:** Dropped the negative-listing runway, fixed the passive ("was created" → "we created"), named the thing.

---

## Example 5: De-slop a Jared post WITHOUT flattening his voice

**Before (slop on top of Jared's bones):**
> "Here's the thing — $1M ARR in 8 weeks isn't really the headline, it's the lesson. The uncomfortable truth is that our customers are genuinely using Watt like a legacy database, and that's okay. Moving forward, we'll navigate this together."

**After (de-slopped, voice intact):**
> "$1M ARR in 8 weeks.
>
> And here's what I'm not satisfied with: our customers are using Watt like a legacy database. That's on us to fix.
>
> We're early. The product's still kinky. But the bet is working.
>
> Let's go."

**Changes:** Killed the em-dash, the "not X, it's Y," the throat-clearing ("the uncomfortable truth is"), the adverbs (really, genuinely), the permission-granting ("and that's okay"), and the jargon ("moving forward," "navigate"). **Kept** the staccato beat, the honest-CEO confession register, the casual number, and the "Let's go" closer. That's the Watt-aware line: strip the tells, keep the craft.
