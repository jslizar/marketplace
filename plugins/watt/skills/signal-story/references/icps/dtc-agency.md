# DTC marketing agency

- **id:** dtc-agency
- **label:** DTC marketing agency
- **reader:** A growth or strategy lead at an agency running paid acquisition and
  retention for direct-to-consumer brands. They pitch and defend client accounts.
- **jtbd:** Hand them a defensible audience angle they can take into a client pitch
  or QBR — proof they can find net-new, high-intent people the client's current
  targeting misses.
- **kpis:** new-business pitch win rate, client retention, the ROAS/CAC they can
  show the client, share-of-wallet across the roster.
- **vocabulary:**
  - *use:* "your client", "the pitch", "net-new reach", "media plan", "activation",
    "prospecting layer", "ROAS", "retention".
  - *avoid:* first-person-brand framing ("our customers", "our spend") — the agency
    is not the brand.
- **objection:** "We already know our client's audience — the targeting is dialled
  in." Preempt it by leading with the *under-tapped* segment and its lift, not the
  obvious core the client already buys.
- **cta:** Take this audience into the next client pitch, or stand it up as a
  net-new prospecting layer alongside the existing campaigns.
- **lead_with:** the net-new reach figure and the highest-lift under-tapped signal
  (the gap) — not the audience the client is already spending against.
- **serves:** dtc-brand — the agency can white-label this and hand it to the brand.
- **evidence_hook:** cross the audience against the client's category (e.g.
  skincare buyers, supplement buyers) to show the overlap with the brand's actual
  customer base. Run only when you want a hard ICP-specific number in the report.
- **example_headline:** "A net-new audience to take to your client"
