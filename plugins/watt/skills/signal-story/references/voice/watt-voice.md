# Watt report voice

The signal story is a Watt deliverable, so its copy carries Watt's voice. This is
the **writing** layer — the register to write in and the words to avoid. AI tells
(em-dashes, "not X, it's Y", jargon, adverbs, throat-clearing) are stripped by the
antislop pass (`${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/skills/watt-antislop/SKILL.md`); this doc
owns the brand register and
vocabulary. Run both over every editorial field before writing the spec.

Source: Watt brand files (brand-principles, founder-voice-lanes, talking-points,
reasons-to-believe, external-expression). Writing only — design briefs omitted.

## The register — three modes, blended to fit the finding

**Break it open — combative, but generous.**
- DO name the incumbents as the category to beat ("the companies winning by
  default," "legacy providers," Google / Meta / the big funds as the hoarders).
- DO make the reader feel the field just shifted in their favor.
- DON'T sound like a charity ("democratizing access").
- DON'T punch down at smaller, named competitors.
- DON'T soften the systemic critique to be polite.

**Compete your heart out — competitive, daring.**
- DO celebrate the brand or builder taking on the incumbents; make winning matter.
- DON'T sound like a motivational poster ("believe in yourself").
- DON'T make it about Watt winning. It's about the reader winning.
- DON'T promise outcomes without proof.

**Real talk — direct, zero pretense.**
- DO admit the limitation before anyone asks: a confound, a thin sample, an
  inflated cross-category lift. The method note is where this lives.
- DO lead with the uncomfortable truth and let the numbers carry it.
- DON'T claim accuracy without showing the math. Every figure traces to the data.
- DON'T be cynical for the sake of edge.

## Words to use carefully — the Salesforce-press-release test

Pause before any of these. Make it earn its place, or cut it:
leverage, synergize, democratize, empower, game-changer, revolutionary,
innovative, disrupt / disruption, thought leader(ship), best-in-class,
world-class, cutting-edge, next-generation, seamless, frictionless, scalable (as a
differentiator), AI-powered (as the main descriptor), data-driven (as a
differentiator), insights (as a product category), "excited to share."
Plus the jargon the antislop swaps for plain words: deep dive, lean into, double
down, circle back, unpack, navigate, moving forward.

"Unlock" is allowed only when it's precise ("the signal that unlocks this
market"), never as vague enablement ("unlock new possibilities").

## Terminology

- It's **signal**, not data. Watt is a **Signal Graph** / **signal
  infrastructure**, never a "data broker." (The positioning shift "data company →
  signal company" is the one contrast the antislop is told to keep.)
- **Show the math.** Lead with the measured number; name the source in the method.
  This is the brand's "let the data speak" and the report's "real numbers only,"
  the same rule.
- Name the big incumbents as a **category**. Never trash a named smaller rival.

## How it lands in a report

The report is confident and specific, not corporate. It states the finding
directly, backs every claim with a measured number, names the incumbents the
reader is up against, and admits what the data can't say. It never reads like a
press release, and it never carries an AI tell.
