# GTM Benchmark Library

Industry rules-of-thumb for B2B GTM. **These are ranges with wide variance** —
use to sanity-check or fill gaps, never as a stand-in for the client's measured
numbers. Compiled from widely-cited industry sources (SaaS benchmark surveys and
sales-operations bodies). **As-of: 2024–2025.** Re-verify time-sensitive rows
(paid CAC, cold-email rates) against a live source before relying on them.

> Adapt for business model. These skew B2B SaaS; for services or physical
> products, treat them as directional and lean harder on the client's own data.

## 1. Sales benchmarks by ACV band

| Metric | Self-serve / PLG (<$5k) | SMB / inside ($5–30k) | Mid-market ($30–100k) | Enterprise (>$100k) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Sales cycle | days–2 wks | 1–6 wks | 1–3 mo | 3–12+ mo |
| Win rate (SQL→won) | n/a (self-serve) | 20–35% | 15–25% | 15–22% |
| CAC payback | <6 mo | 6–12 mo | 12–18 mo | 18–24+ mo |
| Quota : OTE | n/a | 4–5× | 5× | 5–6× |
| Annual quota / AE | n/a | $500–800k | $700k–1.1M | $0.8–1.5M+ |
| SDR : AE ratio | n/a | 0.5–1 : 1 | 1 : 1 | 1.5–2.5 : 1 |
| SE : AE ratio | n/a | 0 | 1 : 3–4 | 1 : 2–3 |
| New-rep ramp | 1–2 mo | 3 mo | 3–6 mo | 6–9 mo |

## 2. Funnel conversion (B2B, directional)

| Stage transition | Typical range |
|---|---|
| Visitor → Lead | 1–3% |
| Lead → MQL | 20–40% |
| MQL → SQL/SAL | 10–30% |
| SQL → Opportunity | 40–60% |
| Opportunity → Closed-Won | 15–30% |
| Overall MQL → Won | 1–5% |
| Free → Paid (PLG, freemium) | 1–5% |
| Free trial → Paid | 10–25% |

## 3. Outbound benchmarks

| Metric | Typical range |
|---|---|
| Cold email open rate | 30–50% (deliverability-dependent) |
| Cold email reply rate | 1–5% |
| Cold email positive-reply rate | 1–3% |
| Cold call connect rate | 5–10% of dials |
| Connect → meeting booked | 10–20% |
| Meetings booked / SDR / month | 10–20 |
| SDR dials / day | 40–60 |

## 4. Channel benchmarks

| Channel | Key benchmark |
|---|---|
| Webinar | Registrants→attendees 35–50%; attendees→SQL 5–15% |
| Content / SEO | 6–12 mo to meaningful organic pipeline; compounding thereafter |
| Paid search | Captures existing intent; CAC rises with competition |
| Paid social | Demand creation; expect higher CAC than search for cold audiences |
| Events / field | Target ≥3–5× pipeline-to-cost; ROI dies without fast follow-up |
| Referral / word-of-mouth | Lowest CAC, highest win rate; cap is volume |
| Partner / channel | Months to activate; trades margin for reach |

## 5. Efficiency & health benchmarks

| Metric | Healthy target |
|---|---|
| Pipeline coverage (open pipeline ÷ quota) | 3–4× |
| LTV : CAC | ≥ 3 : 1 |
| Net revenue retention (NRR) | ≥ 100%; best-in-class 120%+ |
| Gross revenue retention | ≥ 90% |
| Sales magic number | ≥ 0.75 (≥1.0 strong) |
| Rule of 40 (growth % + profit margin %) | ≥ 40 |
| Rep quota attainment (% of reps at/over) | ≥ 60% |
| % revenue from new vs. expansion | varies; expansion-heavy is healthier at scale |

## How to apply

1. Find the client's ACV band in §1; pull cycle, win rate, payback, ratios.
2. Use §2 for funnel-stage assumptions when the client's own conversion data is
   missing.
3. Use §3–§4 to set per-channel targets in the channel plan.
4. Use §5 to judge overall GTM health and to set targets in the recommendations.
5. Always present as a range, name this library as the source, and flag anything
   time-sensitive for live re-verification.
