# Curated people roundup

- **Archetype**: trend claim proven by a roll call of named people, each with their mini-story and stats
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach via the trend take AND every person named.
- **Stage mechanic**: The trend line ("Gen Z is taking over LinkedIn") is the shareable claim; the named roster is the proof and the distribution — each featured person amplifies. Doubles as soft talent scouting.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sugju-choi_gen-z-is-taking-over-linkedin-theyre-ditching-activity-7404577917948866561-uOXa — Sugi Choi (Virio), 773 reactions / 194 comments, 2026-01-10
  - https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7421967518896009216 — Anjali Dhaliwal ("TikTokers are taking over LinkedIn"), 128 reactions / 29 comments, 2026-02-01 (compressed variant: 3 creators + mechanism take)
- **Hook pattern**: "{Group} is taking over {platform}." + one-line why ("They're ditching the corporate fluff, saying what they actually think").
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Trend claim hook + the behavioral why
  2. (Optional) self-aware bridge ("where the rest of us learned to play it safe, they're just being real")
  3. The roster: 3–6 people, each "Name - origin story, what they did, current stat" (followers, role, outcome)
  4. Mechanism take (compressed variant): why the platform actually rewards this
  5. Implication close: what this means for everyone else — no CTA
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Roster entries as short paragraphs with concrete numbers (18k followers, 120k, W25). 150–400 words.
- **Why it works**: Naming people with their real numbers converts an opinion into reporting. Every named person (and their fans) engages; the trend frame gives non-fans a reason to share.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the trend, the roster (people relevant to the client's scene, facts verified this run). Must NOT change: per-person concrete stats — a roster without numbers is name-dropping.
- **Watch-outs**: Featured people must come off flattered, not used — lead each entry with their win. Wrong follower counts get corrected loudly in comments.
