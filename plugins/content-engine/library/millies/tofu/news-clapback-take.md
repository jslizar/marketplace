# News clapback take

- **Archetype**: fresh industry news + a sharpened contrarian or ironic read the coverage missed
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach by riding a live news cycle with a better take than the feed's.
- **Stage mechanic**: The news supplies the attention; the post supplies the angle (irony, contradiction, what-it-really-means). The ICP shares the take, not the news — they already saw the news.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.

- **Hook pattern**: The news compressed into one ironic sentence: "Anthropic just bought a Super Bowl ad to say they'll never run ads." / "Claude just posted a $320k/year ghostwriter position."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. One-line ironic news compression (the whole take is latent in the hook)
  2. The concrete details of the news (the ad's plot, the job listing's terms) — prove you actually looked
  3. The pivot: "here's what makes it really smart / hilarious" — the layer others missed
  4. The underlying principle, argued in 2–3 beats (why this matters beyond the news)
  5. Implication-for-you close or a quoted line that lands the theme — soft comment prompt at most
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Newsy short grafs, one quoted line from the source material, 200–350 words. Ship within 48h of the news or not at all.
- **Why it works**: Newsjacking with actual analysis — the irony compression in line one does the sharing work, the detail block does the credibility work. Timeliness beats polish.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the news item and the angle (must connect to the client's category standing). Must NOT change: hook-as-compressed-irony and the receipts block before the take.
- **Watch-outs**: A take everyone already has is dead on arrival. Verify the news facts THIS run — wrong details in a hot-take post get ratioed. Never name-attack a competitor (see client Canon).
