# Setback-to-breakthrough timeline

- **Archetype**: staccato year-by-year rejection ledger that flips to a win, then universalizes
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP; pure inspiration mechanics, client-agnostic surface.
- **Stage mechanic**: Every ambitious person in the ICP sees their own rejection pile in the ledger. The flip line ("2025: raised $3M+ led by a16z") delivers vicarious payoff; the closing address ("to every founder collecting rejection emails") makes sharing an act of encouragement.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/daniel-dhawan_2019-i-was-rejected-by-yc-for-the-first-activity-7430271916046790656-c8Ao — Daniel Dhawan (Rork), 1,699 reactions / 196 comments, 2026-02-19
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rachael-higgins-becauseofmarketing_2024-close-to-quitting-on-my-business-2025-activity-7413919456810176515-f_Ya — Rachael Higgins (Because of Marketing), 415 reactions / 171 comments, 2026-01-05
- **Hook pattern**: "{year}: {rejection/low point}" repeated as a ledger, ending "{year}: {the win, with a number}". Two-line variant: "2024: close to quitting on my business / 2025: huge breakthrough and made it our most successful year"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. The ledger hook (3–6 dated lines, worst first, win last)
  2. One-line reframe: what the gap actually was ("wasn't luck... 6 years of compounding effort")
  3. (Long variant) the behind-the-scenes chapters: what changed, with sacrifices itemized
  4. Direct address to the reader living the ledger's early years
  5. Aphoristic-but-earned close ("Rejections ≠ the end. Just keep building!") — no CTA
- **Rhythm & formatting**: The ledger IS the format — dates flush left, one line each, no connective tissue. Body (if any) in short bullet-broken bursts. 80–450 words.
- **Why it works**: Pattern interrupt (a wall of dates), earned inspiration (the win is verifiable and dated), and self-recognition — the reader shares it to say something about themselves.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the dates and the specific rejections/wins (must be real and checkable). Must NOT change: worst-first ordering and the single flip line — two wins dilutes it.
- **Watch-outs**: A win too small for the ledger's length falls flat. Invented or fudged dates are existential risk — this format gets fact-checked in the comments.
