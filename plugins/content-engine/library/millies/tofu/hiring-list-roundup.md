# Hiring-list roundup

- **Archetype**: ALL-CAPS utility list of companies matching one screenable trait (just raised / fastest growing / new unicorns), each with a one-line descriptor
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach as pure utility; saves and shares from job seekers AND the listed companies.
- **Stage mechanic**: Double distribution — job seekers save it for the leads, the 30+ named companies amplify because being listed is free press. The trait filter ("raised $10-50M") makes it feel researched, not scraped.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/benmlang_fastest-growing-teams-hiring-list-new-activity-7416870709332803584-qUuA — Ben Lang, 469 reactions / 41 comments, 2026-02-01
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/benmlang_companies-that-just-raised-10-50m-hiring-activity-7413979426264186880-eXTf — Ben Lang, 454 reactions / 44 comments, 2026-02-01
  - Candidates: Ben Lang "$5-10M recently" (279), "new unicorns" (unread, in Millie's bank)
- **Hook pattern**: "{TRAIT} ({HIRING LIST}) — all caps. Second line: 'All {n} of these companies are {trait} and actively hiring:'"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. ALL-CAPS trait + "(HIRING LIST)" hook
  2. One-line scope statement with the count
  3. The list: numbered, each entry "Name - what they do (location)" — 20–40 entries
  4. (Optional) how-to-use or follow prompt at the tail
- **Rhythm & formatting**: The list IS the post. Numbered, ruthlessly parallel one-liners, locations in parens. 300–600 words of pure list.
- **Why it works**: Utility content gets saved (algorithm gold), and every listed company is a potential amplifier. The screening trait is the editorial value-add.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the trait filter (pick one the client's ICP cares about) and every entry (verified this run — funding data goes stale in weeks). Must NOT change: parallel one-line entry format; prose descriptions kill scannability.
- **Watch-outs**: One wrong entry (dead company, wrong stage) and the comments turn on the list's credibility. This format is research-heavy — budget for verification, not writing.
