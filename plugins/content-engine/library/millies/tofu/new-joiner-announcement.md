# New-joiner announcement

- **Archetype**: "I've joined {company}" told as a personal path story, closing on gratitude + an open door
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach through the joiner's own network; employer-brand halo for the company.
- **Stage mechanic**: Career announcements get algorithmic and social lift by default (congrats mechanics). The path story ("my route here wasn't planned") converts a status update into a narrative the wider ICP reads; named founder shoutouts loop in the company's audience.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.

  - Candidate (unread, from Millie's bank): https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jeremyro_im-excited-to-share-that-ive-joined-virio-ugcPost-7381417662683328513-xLuy — Jeremy Ro (Virio)
- **Hook pattern**: "Excited to share that I've joined {company}." then immediately: "My path into {field} wasn't planned at all."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. The announcement, one plain line
  2. The unplanned-path story: 2–3 chapters of how they got here (jobs, pivots, moves)
  3. The conviction beat: the moment this company/city/scene felt right
  4. Named gratitude (founders, people who made it happen)
  5. Open-door close: "if you're working in {space}, would love to connect" — connection-level CTA
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Warm, first-person, plain sentences. 200–350 words. No stats required.
- **Why it works**: LinkedIn's native congrats loop does the distribution; the path story earns strangers. Named shoutouts recruit the company's followers into the comments.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the whole biography (the joiner writes this with you, not about them). Must NOT change: plain-line announcement first, story second — leading with the story buries the news hook.
- **Watch-outs**: This is the joiner's post, not the company's — company voice intruding ("we're thrilled") kills it. Skip if the client can't get the joiner's genuine participation.

## Top example — full post text

Excited to share that I've joined Virio.

My path into startups wasn't planned at all.

I started doing marketing work for a real estate firm, which evolved into partnering with the CEO to build internal tools and eventually, products for the broader proptech industry. I entered the game without knowing what I'm getting myself into.

But those years taught me invaluable lessons. And that's exactly why I moved from Seattle to LA. I wanted to be in the thick of it, learning by doing.

It wasn't until I came to SF that it felt like this is where I need to be.

I thought building my own startups was the ultimate challenge, but being here has shown me there are always new limits to push. Every day brings a new lesson and it's been more exciting than ever.

I'm excited to be working with the Virio team, and special shoutout to co-founders Eric Lay and Emmett Chen-Ran for believing in me and making this opportunity possible.

I am more than thrilled to start this new chapter and to connect with more builders in the city.

If you're working in the GTM space in SF, would love to connect!
