# Dear-teammate letter

- **Archetype**: public open letter to your own co-founder or teammate, epistolary format
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP; culture-signal post that makes the company magnetic.
- **Stage mechanic**: Public vulnerability between co-founders is rare enough to stop the scroll. The ICP (founders, operators) shares it aspirationally — "this is what a working relationship should be." Recruiting halo is the quiet payoff.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sahajgarg_dear-tanay-i-dont-talk-about-this-publicly-activity-7429185333646381056-8Wtr — Sahaj Garg (Wispr), 454 reactions / 28 comments, 2026-02-16
  - Candidate (unread, from Millie's bank): https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kylelacy_lilo4life-share-7454255203848986624-9SWQ — Kyle Lacy, "best marketing leadership teams" tribute
- **Hook pattern**: "Dear {first name}, / I don't talk about this publicly as much as I should: {the tribute thesis}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. "Dear {name}," salutation + confession-of-underexpression hook
  2. The core quality, named concretely (trust, standards) with one behavioral proof
  3. "This might sound unremarkable, but it's rare" — contrast with the failure mode others have
  4. 2–3 short tribute beats, each a paired-opposites observation ("You say yes / I say no")
  5. The hardest-thing-we-survived beat (pivot, crisis) as ultimate proof
  6. Gratitude close + signed "From, {name}" — no CTA
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Letter conventions throughout (salutation, sign-off). Medium paragraphs, one paired-contrast section with line breaks. 300–450 words.
- **Why it works**: Direct-address voyeurism — readers feel they're reading private mail. Specificity of the relationship details makes it unfakeable; the sign-off completes the artifact.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: every relationship detail and the survived-crisis beat. Must NOT change: the letter frame (salutation + sign-off) and the "I don't say this enough" confession hook — without it the post is just praise.
- **Watch-outs**: Reads as performative if the tribute has no friction in it (include a real disagreement or tension). Never use for a person you're currently negotiating with — comments will call it out.

## Top example — full post text

Dear Tanay,

I don't talk about this publicly as much as I should: working with you is one of the best parts of building Wispr.

The foundation of our working relationship is deep trust. When problems arise (and they arise constantly in any startup) there's never a question of whose fault something is. It's always: we both see the issue, now how do we solve it together.

This might sound unremarkable, but it's rare. Most co-founder relationships fracture under stress because when things break, people start assigning blame or questioning each other's judgment. You and I have never fallen into that pattern.

You hold yourself to standards that I've never seen anyone else maintain. If we're going to ask extraordinary things from the people we work with, we have to hold ourselves to even higher standards. You do this consistently, and it sets expectations for the entire company.

What I value most is that you care about what matters beyond just the company. You've been completely supportive of me being present for my family however I need to be, and I appreciate it more than maybe I've been vocal about before. [...]

I'm grateful every day that I get to do this with someone I trust at this level.

From, Sahaj
