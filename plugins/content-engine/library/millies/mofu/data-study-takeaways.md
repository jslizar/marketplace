# Data-study takeaways

- **Archetype**: "we analyzed {huge n}" proprietary-research drop with numbered, stat-dense findings
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the approach credible with evidence nobody else has.
- **Stage mechanic**: Proprietary scale ("1 billion data points, 11 studies") is unmatchable credibility; each numbered finding is a save-worthy fact with an exact figure. Evaluators save and cite it, which is the MOFU win.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thinkingslow_in-the-last-3-months-at-ahrefs-we-analyzed-activity-7424382429542486016--OIQ — Ryan Law (Ahrefs), 2,817 reactions / 265 comments, 2026-02-03
  - (Single-example spec — pin a second when Millie's bank supplies one.)
- **Hook pattern**: "In the last {timeframe} at {company}, we analyzed {enormous n} across {n} studies. Here's what we learned about {topic}:"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Scale hook (timeframe + company + the giant number)
  2. 5–7 numbered findings, each: claim + exact stat + one-line implication ("correlation: 0.737 — stronger than Domain Rating")
  3. Findings ordered surprise-first, and at least one that debunks common practice
  4. (Optional) methodology footnote or link — soft CTA at most
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Numbered findings, decimals kept precise (0.737, 86%, 4x), bolded claim lines optional. 250–450 words.
- **Why it works**: Real research is rare on the feed; precision signals rigor. The debunking finding ("content length has zero correlation") gives practitioners a reason to argue in comments and share to their team.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the study and every number — this format REQUIRES real proprietary data from the client (Proof section or a run-specific study). Must NOT change: exact figures — rounding kills the credibility signal.
- **Watch-outs**: Invented or borrowed data is disqualifying (hard QA block). If the client has no proprietary data, use a different format rather than dressing up public stats.

In the last 3 months at Ahrefs, we analyzed over 1 billion data points across 11 studies*. Here's what we learned about AI search optimization:

1. YouTube mentions are the single strongest predictor of AI visibility (correlation: 0.737) – stronger than Domain Rating, backlinks, or any traditional SEO factor. YouTube is heavily cited in AI responses, and both Google and OpenAI train on YouTube content.

2. For a given query, AI Mode and AI Overviews reach the same conclusions 86% of the time – but cite almost entirely different sources (only 13.7% citation overlap). AI Mode responses are 4x longer and mention 3x more entities.

3. Content length has essentially zero correlation with AI citations (0.04). 53% of all AI Overview citations go to pages under 1,000 words. Writing ultra-long content isn't necessary for AI visibility.

4. Google still sends 345x more traffic than ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity combined – but ChatGPT accounts for 80%+ of all AI-driven website traffic.

5. AI Overviews have a 70% chance of changing from one observation to the next, with content lasting an average of just 2.15 days. But semantic meaning stays remarkably consistent (0.95 cosine similarity).

6. "Best X" blog lists make up 43.8% of all page types cited in ChatGPT responses. 35% of those lists come from low-authority domains.
