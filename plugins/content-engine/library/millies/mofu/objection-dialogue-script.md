# Objection dialogue script

- **Archetype**: fictional two-speaker argument (practitioner vs. skeptical boss) that dismantles an orthodoxy objection by objection
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent by staging the exact argument the evaluator is having internally.
- **Stage mechanic**: The dialogue format lets the post voice AND defeat every objection the ICP's own CEO/board would raise (leads! MQLs! metrics!). Readers forward it to the person they're arguing with — built-in distribution to the decision-maker.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chrisclickup_head-of-marketing-were-ungating-all-of-activity-7423017247788474368-BNDb — Chris Cunningham (ClickUp), 1,076 reactions / 161 comments, 2026-01-30
  - (Single-example spec — pin a second when Millie's bank supplies one.)
- **Hook pattern**: "{Role}: {the heresy, stated as a decision}. / {Boss role}: Have you completely lost your mind? That's {the sacred metric}!"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Two-line dialogue hook: the heresy + the outraged objection
  2. 5–8 objection/rebuttal rounds, escalating from tactical to strategic; each rebuttal carries a concrete number or named consequence
  3. The strongest objection saved for late ("the board tracks MQLs") answered with the reframe ("the board tracks revenue")
  4. Skeptic's grudging pivot or unresolved beat — the reader finishes the argument
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Pure script — "Role:" prefixes, no narration. Numbers embedded in dialogue. 250–400 words.
- **Why it works**: Argument-as-theater is more persuasive than assertion; the reader rehearses winning their own internal debate. Fiction gives cover for spicier claims than a first-person post could make.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the orthodoxy under attack (one the client has standing on) and the numbers in rebuttals (from the client's Proof or cited sources). Must NOT change: escalation order and the sacred-metric objection near the end.
- **Watch-outs**: Both voices must be steel-manned — a strawman boss makes the ICP defensive, not persuaded. Keep it clearly archetypal; naming a real company's exec as the fool is a hard block.

## Top example — full post text

Head of Marketing: We're ungating all of our content.
CEO: Have you completely lost your mind? That's 2,000 leads per quarter!
Head of Marketing: 2,000 fake emails from people who will never buy.

Meanwhile, our actual buyers can't share our content with their team because it's locked behind a form.

CEO: We need leads for Sales.

Head of Marketing: Sales hates these leads. Ask them. Jessica told me yesterday she'd rather have 10 people who actually read our content than 1,000 who used burner emails for a download.

CEO: The board tracks MQLs.

Head of Marketing: The board tracks revenue. Our competitors ungated everything last year. Their traffic 5x'd. Their sales cycles dropped 20%. Why? Because entire buying committees could actually access their content.

CEO: But how do we know who's interested?

Head of Marketing: Intent data. If someone reads 5 articles, watches 2 videos, and visits pricing, they're interested. If someone downloads one PDF and ghosts, they're not.

CEO: This is insane.

Head of Marketing: You know what's insane? Watching our gated content get 500 views while our competitor's free guide gets 50,000 and becomes the industry standard.

CEO: That sounds like a content problem...

Head of Marketing: No. It's a distribution problem because it doesn't have the chance to actually get shared.

CEO: Okay. But marketing will have no metrics.
