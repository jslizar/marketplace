# Jargon cheat sheet

- **Archetype**: "the jargon is the problem" + a decoder cheat sheet for the terms gatekeeping your ICP
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes a hidden problem (you can't challenge what you don't understand) urgent, then solves it on the spot.
- **Stage mechanic**: Names the ICP's quiet embarrassment (nodding along to ROAS/MER/CAC) without shaming them — the agency/vendor is the villain. The cheat sheet is instant utility; the closing question drives comments. High share-to-reaction ratio (47 shares on 206 reactions).
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.

  - (Single-example spec — pin a second when Millie's bank supplies one.)
- **Hook pattern**: "{Field} has become full of jargon. And that's a problem."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Jargon-is-the-problem hook, two short lines
  2. The victim: who's being kept in the dark (the ICP, sympathetically drawn), and the villain: who benefits (vendors, agencies)
  3. The acronym wall, rendered literally (ROAS. MER. CAC. LTV.) — one per line
  4. The stakes: what not-understanding costs ("difficult to challenge strategy, ask the right questions")
  5. The asset: "I put together this cheat sheet covering {n} terms" + 3–5 sample definitions with real teeth
  6. Comment-question close ("which term do you think marketers misuse most?") — soft CTA
- **Rhythm & formatting**: One-word-per-line acronym wall is the signature visual. Definitions as "Term → why it matters" pairs. 250–400 words, usually with an attached carousel/image.
- **Why it works**: Flatters the reader's suspicion (the jargon IS obfuscation), delivers immediate utility, and positions the author as the translator — the trusted-advisor MOFU position.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the field's jargon set and the sample definitions (client's domain). Must NOT change: villain-is-the-vendor framing and the acronym wall.
- **Watch-outs**: Definitions must be sharp enough that experts nod — a wrong or mushy definition inverts the authority play. Don't punch at named competitors.

## Top example — full post text

Performance marketing has become full of jargon.

And that's a problem.

I speak to a lot of founders and brand managers at premium and luxury brands who are investing serious budgets into Meta, TikTok and Google, yet they're often being presented with reports full of acronyms that nobody has properly explained.

ROAS.
MER.
CAC.
LTV.
NCROAS.

If you don't understand what these actually mean, it's incredibly difficult to challenge strategy, ask the right questions, or know whether your agency is genuinely growing the business.

So I put together this cheat sheet covering 15 performance marketing terms every luxury brand manager should know.

A few of the most important:

MER → Probably the most overlooked profitability metric in modern marketing.

CAC vs LTV → The relationship that determines whether your growth is sustainable.

Incrementality → The difference between ads creating sales and simply claiming credit for them.

Creative Fatigue → One of the biggest reasons performance drops over time.

Creative as a Growth Lever → Increasingly, the quality of your creative has more impact than campaign settings.

The reality is that performance marketing isn't won through platform hacks anymore.

It's won through: Better creative. Better measurement. Better commercial understanding.

Luxury brands that understand these fundamentals make significantly better marketing decisions.

Which term do you think marketers misuse or misunderstand the most?
