# Platform-mechanics teardown

- **Archetype**: "I've spent ${huge n} on {channel}" + why the conventional wisdom breaks on a specific platform, mechanism by mechanism, ending in a gated deep-dive
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem (your playbook doesn't transfer) urgent, and the author's mechanism-level understanding credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: Spend-level credibility ("$100M on ads") plus a counterintuitive claim ("the best Meta ads always fail on AppLovin") forces practitioners to check their own assumptions. The mechanism explanation is free; the full guide is gated — clean MOFU capture.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.

  - (Single-example spec — pin a second when Millie's bank supplies one.)
- **Hook pattern**: "I've spent ${n} on {channel}. The best {channel A} {assets} always fail on {channel B}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Spend-credibility hook + the counterintuitive failure claim
  2. The mechanism contrast, arrow-bulleted: how channel A works vs. how channel B works (user behavior level)
  3. The tactical consequence: what to do differently, step by step ("the first 3s are irrelevant... hit them with your strongest CTA at exactly the right time")
  4. The gated asset: "I spent the week creating a full guide" + contents list
  5. Gate close: connect + comment keyword
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Arrow bullets throughout, second-person imperatives in the tactical block, contents list for the gate. 250–400 words.
- **Why it works**: Mechanism-level explanation (WHY the platform behaves differently) reads as expertise, not opinion. The spend number is the unfakeable badge; the gate harvests exactly the evaluators who need the depth.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the channels, the spend number (Canon-verifiable), the mechanism, the guide. Must NOT change: mechanism-before-tactics ordering and the free/gated value split.
- **Watch-outs**: The mechanism claim will be tested by practitioners in the comments — it must be technically right. A spend number the client can't back is a hard QA block.

I've spent $100M on ads

The best Meta ads always fail on AppLovin.

It's because of how ads work on AppLovin

Facebook & Instagram
→ You are interrupting the scroll
→ The 3s hook is everything

AppLovin (& reward video channels)
→ The user cannot skip for 15-30 seconds
→ They watch to get whatever in-game reward they've been promised (extra lives, bonuses, in-game currencies)

If your user cannot skip, the first 3s are irrelevant…
→ They're annoyed they had to watch the ad
→ So tell a story / show a satisfying product demo.
→ Build a narrative arc that doesn't feel like a hard sell
→ Entertain them while they wait.

But what happens in the last 3s before they can play again?
→ They're watching your ad
→ Wait until just before they can close the ad
→ Hit them with your strongest CTA at exactly the right time
→ E.g. "Get 50% Off Now - "Claim Your One-Time Bundle."
