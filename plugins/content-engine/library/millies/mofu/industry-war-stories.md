# Industry war stories

- **Archetype**: profane love letter to an industry via a curated list of unbelievable-but-true anecdotes
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — builds insider credibility with the ICP; you can only tell these stories if you're really in the room.
- **Stage mechanic**: The anecdotes prove the author is deep in the industry's social fabric (dinners with brokers). The ICP shares it for the laughs; the close ("a lot is changing in logistics") quietly plants the company's thesis.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jesse-buckingham-79649a26_freight-is-a-fking-crazy-industry-i-was-activity-7417931389750263808-4BKl — Jesse Buckingham (Vooma), 232 reactions / 52 comments (44 "funny"), 2026-01-16
  - (Single-example spec — pin a second when Millie's bank supplies one.)
- **Hook pattern**: "{Industry} is a f**king crazy industry. I was at {insider setting} and asked for their best {industry} stories."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Profanity-softened hook + the insider setting that sourced the stories
  2. 3–5 arrow-bulleted anecdotes, each a complete miniature story with an escalating absurdity (kitten → mountain lion)
  3. Anecdotes ordered for rhythm: funny → funnier → operatic
  4. The turn: "A lot is changing in {industry}." — one line
  5. Affection close: why this frontier attracts "the most interesting people doing the wildest sh*t" — no CTA
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Arrow bullets, each anecdote 2–4 sentences, censored profanity (f**king) as tonal signal. 200–350 words.
- **Why it works**: Entertainment earns the reach; provenance ("I was at a dinner") earns the insider credibility that MOFU needs. The affectionate close makes the industry feel chosen, which flatters the ICP.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the industry and the anecdotes (really collected, not invented — comments will add their own, which is the point). Must NOT change: the escalation ordering and the one-line thesis turn before the close.
- **Watch-outs**: Anecdotes that mock customers kill it — the tone is "can you believe this glorious chaos," never contempt. Profanity level must match the client's voice rules (check Canon).

## Top example — full post text

Freight is a f**king crazy industry. I was at a dinner with a few brokers the other night and asked them for their best freight stories. Some of the funniest ones I heard:

→ A driver whose company wouldn't let him have pets in the truck. He found a lost kitten on the road and pushed for approval to keep it in the cab. Pulled into the terminal 3 months later with a full-grown mountain lion sitting in his cab. Not a kitten it turns out. He wouldn't give it up.

→ A driver who drove his truck into the middle of a forest, parked it, and texted his dispatcher: "I quit." The broker had to call the local sheriff to find the truck in a rogue forest in the Midwest.

→ A broker who had a load stolen but had tracking on it. They figured out where the truck was parked, broke into it, requisitioned an excavator out the back, and pulled off a full vigilante rescue mission to recover the freight.

→ A tamper-proof trailer with seals on the back to prevent theft. Instead of breaking the seals, someone removed the entire back of the trailer to pull the freight out and load it onto a railcar.

A lot is changing in logistics.

But it's the frontier of American entrepreneurship and I'm certain it will continue to attract the most interesting people doing the wildest sh*t.
