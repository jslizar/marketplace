# Batch hotlist teardown

- **Archetype**: ranked spotlight of a cohort's hottest companies, with insider color on the founders behind each
- **Lane**: ABM — targets a whole accelerator batch / cohort of accounts, founder by founder.
- **Stage mechanic**: Ranking ("one of YC's most popular in-batch startups, 2.7M monthly visitors") plus human detail ("wore a pirate eyepatch as a kid", "performed a 25-min Rachmaninov concerto") makes every featured founder feel SEEN, not scraped. They engage, their batchmates engage, and the author becomes the chronicler of the cohort they sell into.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jeremyro_you-ever-wonder-whos-behind-y-combinators-activity-7424551753334808577-5gU_ — Jeremy Ro (Virio), 447 reactions / 87 comments, 2026-02-03
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/chris-pisarski_pax-historia-is-one-of-ycs-most-popular-activity-7423089371639095296-RpBo — Chris Pisarski (Crustdata), 437 reactions / 56 comments, 2026-02-05
- **Hook pattern**: "You ever wonder who's behind {cohort}'s hottest {segment} startups?" / "{Company} is one of {cohort}'s most popular startups this year with {traffic/metric}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Curiosity hook naming the cohort + a scale metric
  2. Scope line ("These are the {n} founders building real {segment} tech")
  3. Numbered company entries: what it does in one line, then 2–4 founder bullets mixing the impressive with the human (quant offer dropped, Reddit-sourced co-founder, eyepatch)
  4. (Metric variant) the traffic/record numbers that justify the ranking
  5. Runs long; ends mid-roster energy — the list is the close
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Numbered entries, founder bullets with dashes, human details unpolished on purpose. 400–700 words.
- **ABM payoff**: Each featured founder gets tag-worthy personal press; the author earns DM-open status with an entire batch — the exact accounts an early-stage vendor sells into.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the cohort (the client's target segment) and every founder detail (researched, true, flattering-but-specific). Must NOT change: the impressive+human pairing per founder — metrics alone reads as a database dump.
- **Watch-outs**: Personal details must be public/consented and affectionate — one creepy-feeling detail poisons the whole format. Verify "hottest" claims with real numbers.
