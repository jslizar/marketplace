# Mafia map

- **Archetype**: alumni-network funding map — "the {company} Mafia has raised ${total}" with the full founder-by-founder ledger
- **Lane**: ABM — glorifies one anchor company AND every alumni-founded company in a single post.
- **Stage mechanic**: The anchor company amplifies (their culture made the mafia); every listed founder amplifies (they're canonized); the data provider gets a named credit (Crustdata) — three concentric rings of targets served at once. The ledger's completeness makes it a reference document that gets cited for months.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/feed/update/urn:li:activity:7409946902818967552 — Chris Pisarski (Crustdata), 986 reactions / 33 comments, 2025-12-29
  - (Single-example spec — pin a second when Millie's bank supplies one.)
- **Hook pattern**: "The {Company} Mafia has raised over ${total} in total funding."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Mafia hook with the aggregate number
  2. The culture thesis: what the anchor company trained its people to do (2–3 lines, specific capabilities)
  3. "That culture compounds." — the one-line bridge
  4. Methodology flex: how the map was built (names the client's own product as the instrument — the quiet BOFU sell)
  5. The ledger: "Company - $raised - Founder names", sorted by raise, 15–30 entries
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Ledger lines ruthlessly parallel; dollar amounts exact ($641M, $77.2M). 300–500 words, mostly ledger.
- **ABM payoff**: Anchor-company leadership reshares it as employer-brand proof; listed founders engage; and the methodology beat demos the client's product to everyone reading. Triple-purpose post.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the anchor company (pick one upstream of the client's ICP) and the full ledger (verified funding data, current). The methodology beat should name the client's actual tooling only if true. Must NOT change: aggregate-number hook and exact per-company figures.
- **Watch-outs**: Funding figures get fact-checked instantly — source them this run. Missing a famous alum is the top comment risk (which is also engagement — but cap the misses).
