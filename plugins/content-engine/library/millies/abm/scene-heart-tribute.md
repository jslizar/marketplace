# Scene heart tribute

- **Archetype**: love letter to a city/scene that name-checks every company in it ("List ABM")
- **Lane**: ABM — targets an entire ecosystem of accounts at once; every named company is a warm-open.
- **ABM mechanic**: Declaring a place "the beating heart of {industry}" and rostering its companies flatters dozens of targets simultaneously. Each named brokerage/carrier engages, follows, or DMs — and the author announced they're physically visiting, converting engagement into meetings that week. The "(Who did I miss?!)" line farms comments from unlisted accounts begging in.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jesse-buckingham-79649a26_chattanooga-is-the-beating-heart-of-freight-activity-7401983816355282944-zC8Q — Jesse Buckingham (Vooma), 180 reactions / 11 comments, 2025-12-03
  - Candidate (unread, from Millie's bank): alexbaldovin "Europe is becoming the Clay capital of the world"
- **Hook pattern**: "{Place} is the beating heart of {industry}." / "{Region} is becoming the {category} capital of the world."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. The crowning hook (place + industry superlative)
  2. The evidence line: density claim with a comparison ("outside of Chicago, you won't find a higher density of...")
  3. The why-now: the author's presence or event in the scene this week (the ABM tell — meetings are the goal)
  4. The roster: companies grouped by category, each with name + one-line identity — 10–25 entries
  5. "(Who did I miss?!)" comment farm
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Roster as bolded group headers + "Name – descriptor" lines. 250–500 words. Roster accuracy is the whole game.
- **ABM payoff**: Every listed company's team sees it, likes it, and knows the author is in town; unlisted companies self-identify in comments. Warm context for every meeting on the trip.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the scene and the roster (client's real target geography/segment; every entry verified). Must NOT change: the visit/why-now beat and the who-did-I-miss close.
- **Watch-outs**: Wrong or missing major players embarrasses the author with the exact audience being courted. Roster research is the work — budget accordingly.
