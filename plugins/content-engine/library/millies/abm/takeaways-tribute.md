# Takeaways tribute

- **Archetype**: "my biggest takeaways from {person}" — numbered wisdom post that credits one named expert throughout
- **Lane**: ABM — glorifies a single named person (and their company) by canonizing their thinking.
- **ABM mechanic**: The featured person gets the ultimate compliment: their ideas, organized and amplified to a large audience with full credit. They (and their company) reshare it. Works as pre-meeting warm-up, podcast promotion, or pure relationship capital with a strategic account.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: curated from Millie's bank; refreshed only when the bank updates.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lennyrachitsky_my-biggest-takeaways-from-elena-verna-head-activity-7407893643732316162-v6OE — Lenny Rachitsky (on Elena Verna, Lovable), 3,902 reactions / 207 comments, 2025-12-19
  - (Single-example spec — pin a second when Millie's bank supplies one.)
- **Hook pattern**: "My biggest takeaways from {person} ({role} at {company}):"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Direct hook naming the person, role, company
  2. 5–8 numbered takeaways; each: bold claim line → 2–4 lines of explanation with the person's specific numbers ("only 30% to 40% of what she learned in 20 years still applies", "optimization drives about 5% of their growth")
  3. Takeaways ordered most-paradigm-breaking first
  4. (Optional) link to the full conversation — soft CTA
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Numbered, claim-then-evidence structure, the subject's own stats doing the work. 300–500 words.
- **ABM payoff**: The featured person owes the author reach; their company gets thought-leadership press. When the author's company sells into that account (or its peers), the door is warm.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must change: the person (a target account's exec, an ecosystem kingmaker) and the takeaways (from a real conversation, talk, or their public content — sourced). Must NOT change: full credit throughout — the author is curator, not co-author.
- **Watch-outs**: Paraphrasing the person wrong is the fatal error — verify each takeaway against the source. If takeaways read as generic, the tribute deflates; keep their weirdest, most specific claims.

## Top example — full post text

My biggest takeaways from Elena Verna (Head of Growth at Lovable):

1. In AI, you now need to find product-market fit every three months. Product-market fit used to mean: build something people want, then scale it for years. In AI, the underlying technology changes so fast—and customer expectations with it—that you're constantly re-earning that fit. Even at $200M ARR.

2. The growth playbook has fundamentally changed for AI companies. Elena has led growth at Miro, Dropbox, and Amplitude and advised dozens more companies on growth. At Lovable, she says only 30% to 40% of what she learned in 20 years still applies.

3. At Lovable, growth is driven mostly through new features, not optimizing funnels. At the fastest-growing company in history, optimization drives about 5% of their growth. The other 95% comes from launching new features and products. Small tweaks don't move the needle when everything is changing.
