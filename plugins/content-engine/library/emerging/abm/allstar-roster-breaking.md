# All-star roster breaking

- **Archetype**: credit-roll
- **Funnel stage**: ABM (BOFU by definition) — glorifies a named account so its people amplify it.
- **ABM mechanic**: Frames the target company's hiring as assembling "the Real Madrid of AI" — a status analogy — then credit-rolls named hires with their prestigious origins. Every person listed is a potential amplifier; the closing question stokes rivalry-driven comments.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan; rolls into Virio next Saturday)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/guillermoflor_breaking-anthropic-is-building-the-real-activity-7482526899559325696-24av — Guillermo Flor, 372 reactions / 48 comments, 2026-07-13 (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "BREAKING: {company} is building the {legendary team} of {industry}"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. BREAKING hook + status analogy
  2. Reframe line (not just X — assembling the all-star team)
  3. Scope-setter (timeframe + breadth)
  4. Bulleted credit roll — name (prestigious origin) per line
  5. Rivalry question ("Who's missing? Can {rival} win now?")
  6. DON'T MISS resource links
- **Rhythm & formatting**: All-caps markers (BREAKING, DON'T MISS), • bullets with parenthetical origins, links at the bottom. ~140 words.
- **Why it works**: Borrowed status stacks twice — the analogy elevates the company, the origins elevate each hire. Named people share posts that celebrate them; the rivalry question manufactures debate.
- **Adaptation notes**: Swap in the target account's real hires/wins (this run's research, with sources). The analogy must fit the industry's shared reference points.
- **Watch-outs**: A roster of unknowns has no borrowed status — this format needs genuinely recognizable names or verifiable prestige markers.
