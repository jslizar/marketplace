# Office-tour spotlight series

- **Archetype**: company-spotlight
- **Funnel stage**: ABM — glorifies a named account by documenting its team and space; the series format makes the next target ask to be featured.
- **Stage mechanic**: Launches a named, hashtagged series whose entire premise is visiting and celebrating specific companies. Episode 1 anoints the first account (what they do, framed generously), the series promise creates a slot every target wants, and the close ("Who should I visit next?") turns nominations into engagement and pipeline.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan, second pass — keyword sweep)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sharoniroezi_officetourswithsharon-officetour-techinafrica-activity-7480945728157220865-fOAu — Sharon Iroezi, 372 reactions / 38 comments, 2026-07-09, video (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "✨ Introducing a new series:\n#{SeriesNameWithAuthor}"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Series-launch hook + branded hashtag
  2. The curiosity credential (why the author cares about these companies)
  3. 📍 Episode 1: named account + generous one-paragraph what-they-do
  4. The series promise (who gets visited, what gets shared)
  5. Nomination CTA ("Who should I visit next?")
  6. Hashtag block
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Short blocks, one emoji per structural beat (✨ launch, 📍 episode), video carries the tour; text stays under 700 chars.
- **Why it works**: The featured account reposts it (their office, their story), the account's employees engage, and every future target has a reason to raise a hand — an ABM flywheel disguised as a content series.
- **Adaptation notes**: Works for a client targeting a niche ecosystem: name the series after the wedge (region, vertical, tool stack) and make Episode 1 the account with the warmest relationship.
- **Watch-outs**: Episode 1 sets the bar — pick an account whose story photographs well. The nomination close only works if the author actually follows through on episode 2.
