# Campaign handoff anatomy

- **Archetype**: campaign-anatomy
- **Funnel stage**: ABM (BOFU by definition) — glorifies a named campaign so the team behind it amplifies.
- **ABM mechanic**: A first-party campaign recap that rides a cultural moment (the World Cup) into the brand's own launch ("Thanks World. We'll Take It From Here."). The receipts list + team credit makes everyone involved a sharer; outside voices can run the same anatomy on a target's campaign as tribute.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan; rolls into Virio next Saturday)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/radhikaduggal_one-month-ago-the-fifa-world-cup-kicked-activity-7482463872331767808-csoD — Radhika Duggal (CMO, MLS), 382 reactions / 27 comments, 2026-07-13 (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "One month ago, {cultural moment} kicked off, creating {superlative}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Moment-anchor hook (timeframe + significance)
  2. Mission line — the simple goal everyone rallied around
  3. Receipts list — "we've:" + 3 concrete accomplishment bullets with numbers
  4. The launch reveal — campaign name in quotes as its own beat
  5. Emotional why (what the moment reminded everyone)
  6. Invitation close + team credit ("Proud of the incredible team behind this")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Dash bullets for receipts, campaign name quoted, warm rallying tone. ~200 words.
- **Why it works**: Borrowed momentum — the campaign inherits the cultural moment's energy. The receipts prove work happened; the team credit turns the whole org into distribution.
- **Adaptation notes**: As tribute: swap first person for "the team at {target}" and cite this run's research. The campaign name reveal must land as its own paragraph.
- **Watch-outs**: Without concrete receipts (numbers, named outputs) it reads as a press release. The cultural moment must genuinely connect to the campaign.

## Top example — full post text

```
One month ago, the FIFA World Cup kicked off in North America, creating one of the biggest moments in our sport's history. Since then, it's been inspiring to see our League, Clubs, players and partners come together around a simple goal: connect Major League Soccer to the excitement of the World Cup and invite new fans into our game.

Over the past month, we've:
-Welcomed millions of fans to Soccer Celebration viewing events across our Club markets.
-Shared the stories of the 45 MLS players competing on the world stage.
-Featured MLS voices in hundreds of media interviews, helping connect World Cup conversations back to our League.

Today, we launch the final phase of that journey with our new campaign: "Thanks World. We'll Take It From Here."

The World Cup has reminded all of us what makes soccer so powerful: the joy, the passion, the sense of belonging, and the way it brings people together. Our hope is simple: if you fell in love with that feeling this summer, come experience it with us. Because that same energy exists every time MLS kicks off.

Proud of the incredible team behind this campaign, and excited for what's next!
```
