# Pivot-proof milestone

- **Archetype**: pivot-case-study
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU — converts existing intent.
- **Stage mechanic**: A milestone announcement (round closed in 36 hours) that smuggles in a full case study: 7 months of struggle → one strategic flip → 20x revenue → profitability. The struggle-first honesty de-risks the reader's own decision; the P.S. converts.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan; rolls into Virio next Saturday)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aleckremins_excited-to-announce-we-just-closed-our-latest-activity-7480969240314011648-fIOD — Alec Kremins, 398 reactions / 154 comments, 2026-07-09 (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "Excited to announce {milestone} in {startlingly short time}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Milestone hook with a speed/scale stat
  2. Gratitude line (names the backers)
  3. The struggle admission (specific duration)
  4. The flip — one quotable line ("software as a service → service as software")
  5. Result stat + customer-outcome line
  6. Pre-empted objection ("profitable, so why raise?")
  7. Credit roll to named allies
  8. P.S. — direct CTA to the ICP
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Short paragraphs, one wordplay pivot line, named people, a P.S. CTA. ~190 words.
- **Why it works**: Struggle-before-success reads as honesty, not bragging; the quotable flip line gives commenters something to repeat; the P.S. catches readers at peak belief.
- **Adaptation notes**: Every number and name from the client's Proof/Canon only. The flip line must be the client's actual strategic insight, compressed to a chiasmus if possible.
- **Watch-outs**: Without the struggle block it's just a funding announcement (ABM-adjacent, weaker BOFU). Fabricated speed stats are a hard QA block.

## Top example — full post text

```
Excited to announce we just closed our latest funding round in 36 hours.

Thanks to the incredible support from Lightbank & Verissimo Ventures.

For 7 months, we struggled to win customers, and struggled even more to keep them happy.

In the past 3 months, we 20’xd revenue by flipping the script.

We switched from software as a service to service as software.

The more important part is the repeatable results our customers are seeing.

We just hit profitability, so why raise?

Because there’s a massive opportunity to 100x what we’re building, and I’d rather move now than wish I had later.

Massive shoutout to Alex Oppenheimer at Verissimo and Eric Ong at LightBank.

These two aren’t just incredible investors, but mentors and friends who got the vision immediately.

They are allies I can count on when it matters most.

The stories worth telling are just getting started.

All glory to God.

P.S. if you’re a founder or exec sitting on a story worth telling, let’s chat 🤘
```
