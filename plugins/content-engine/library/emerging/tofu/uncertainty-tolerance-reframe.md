# Uncertainty tolerance reframe

- **Archetype**: reframe-manifesto
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — broad relatable truth, no hard ask.
- **Stage mechanic**: Takes a familiar success variable (talent, strategy, timing) and swaps in an unexpected one (tolerance for not knowing), then closes on a quotable two-liner. Pure reach: the reframe is the product.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan, second pass — author-seeded)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sahilbloom_success-is-not-about-knowing-the-answer-activity-7482041108420616192-gLAS — Sahil Bloom, 3,520 reactions / 419 comments, 2026-07-12 (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "{Outcome} is not about {expected cause}.\n\n(It's about {unexpected cause}.)"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Two-line reframe hook, parenthetical payoff
  2. Name the conventional candidates ("Everyone talks about X. Y. Z.")
  3. The real separator, one line
  4. Expand the reframed trait in 2–3 short beats
  5. "Here's what I've learned" turn
  6. Contrast: who wins vs. who bails
  7. Aphorism close ("Uncertainty is not the enemy… it's the entrance fee.")
  8. Boilerplate follow/repost + lead-magnet PS (optional)
- **Rhythm & formatting**: One-line paragraphs, escalating repetition ("One more month. One more quarter. One more year."), lands on a two-word imperative. ~180 words before the PS.
- **Why it works**: The reframe flatters the reader (persistence beats talent) while feeling contrarian; the aphorism close begs the repost.
- **Adaptation notes**: The reframed variable must be earned by the client's actual story — swap in a trait their Canon supports.
- **Watch-outs**: Without a concrete expansion (what "tolerating fog" looks like), it collapses into a motivational poster.

## Top example — full post text

```
Success is not about knowing the answer.

(It's about tolerating not having one.)

Everyone talks about talent. Strategy. Timing.

But the older I get, the more I realize the real separator is much simpler.

It's tolerance for not knowing.

Not knowing if it will work. Not knowing when it will click. Not knowing if the effort is adding up to anything.

That feeling makes most people bail.

They don't fail. They just stop tolerating the fog.

Here's what I've learned:

The people who win are rarely the smartest or the most talented.

They're the ones who can sit in uncertainty the longest without flinching.

While everyone else is running back to safety, they stay in the discomfort.

One more month. One more quarter. One more year.

Not because they know something others don't.

Because they can stand not knowing longer than others can.

Uncertainty is not the enemy of success.

It's the entrance fee.

Learn to tolerate it.
```
