# Objection-rebuttal gauntlet

- **Archetype**: objection-rebuttal
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP.
- **Stage mechanic**: Opens with a shock stat framed as a question, then stages the comment section's objections as quoted strawmen and knocks each down in two lines. Readers share it to win arguments they've already had.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan; rolls into Virio next Saturday)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/erinmcgoff_did-you-know-nearly-every-single-country-activity-7482506718585954304-oqpu — Erin McGoff, 328 reactions / 15 comments, 2026-07-13 (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "Did you know {shock stat}… except {glaring exception}?"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Shock-stat question hook
  2. One-line qualifier (pre-empt the well-actually crowd)
  3. Objection № 1 in quotes → 2–4 line rebuttal → "Next."
  4. Repeat for 3–4 objections, strongest last
  5. Grounding stat with source
  6. Constructive close — solutions exist + rhetorical dare
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Quoted objections as their own paragraphs, staccato one-word transitions ("Next."), a cited stat. ~230 words.
- **Why it works**: It does the reader's arguing for them. Pre-empting objections inside the post starves the comment section of gotchas and converts it into agreement.
- **Adaptation notes**: Works for any status-quo the client challenges. Objections must be the REAL ones the ICP voices, phrased the way they say them.
- **Watch-outs**: Strawmanning weak objections kills credibility — rebut the strongest version. Keep it business-relevant or it drifts into pure politics.

## Top example — full post text

```
Did you know nearly *every single country on earth* has a paid maternity leave policy… except the United States?

Yes we have state and company programs but federally, there's nothing on the books. 

“I’m not going to pay people to not work!”

You already do.

Unemployment. 
Social security. 
Disability. 

Next. 

“Companies won’t hire women who will just get pregnant and they’ll have to pay double to replace them while on leave.”

In no country is 100% of the burden on the company. Also, paid parental leave (both parents) is vital. Otherwise the backfire effect will negatively harm women. 

“That’s socialism.”

No, that’s how taxes are supposed to work. They’re supposed to better your life and community. I’d rather my tax dollars go to this than many of the other things they’re currently going to. 

“Our current system works.”

1 in 4 women go back to work within 2 weeks of giving birth (source: MomsFirstUS) How is that working? 

Plenty of states from Maine to Oregon are enacting their own paid leave policies. But there is still no baseline for the most vulnerable families. 

This doesn’t need to be complicated, but it does need to be addressed. Expanding unemployment or SDTI. Introducing insurance legislation. Passing tax credits. There are creative solutions - and surely the ‘greatest country on earth’ can figure it out. Right?
```
