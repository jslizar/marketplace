# Contrarian strength anthem

- **Archetype**: contrarian-take
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP.
- **Stage mechanic**: A polarizing-but-safe leadership stance ("not everyone has to like you") that readers repost to signal their own backbone. The nuance twist (strong ≠ jerk) makes it shareable by both camps.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan; rolls into Virio next Saturday)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/rachelmitchell333_rachelmitchell-ladylongevity-businessdevelopment-activity-7480981372216614912-Z06N — Rachel Mitchell, 2,890 reactions / 363 comments, 2026-07-09 (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "Unpopular opinion: {norm-violating stance}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. "Unpopular opinion" hook + stance
  2. Immediate nuance guard (strong, not rude)
  3. Principle blocks — short aphoristic pairs (stand for nothing / stand for something)
  4. Personal war story (one paragraph, insider texture)
  5. All-caps quotable aphorism
  6. Universal close — no CTA
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 1–2 line paragraphs, one ⚠️ callout, signed off with the author's name. ~230 words.
- **Why it works**: Permission + identity signaling. The aphorism block ("wolf vs donkey") is a built-in screenshot moment; the nuance guard widens the agreeable audience instead of splitting it.
- **Adaptation notes**: Swap the stance for the client's held belief; the war story must be genuinely theirs (Canon only). Keep the all-caps aphorism — it's the engine.
- **Watch-outs**: A contrarian take everyone already agrees with reads as engagement bait. The stance must cost something.

## Top example — full post text

```
Unpopular opinion:

Not everyone has to like you. But you don’t have to be a “jerk” to be strong. Wolf”s are strong, and calm, and quiet at times.

Some of the most respected leaders, founders, and innovators have one thing in common. 

They made decisions based on principles, not popularity.

If you spend your life trying to please everyone, you'll eventually stop being yourself.

People who stand for nothing are rarely criticized.

People who stand for something almost always are.

⚠️But this does not mean be a “jerk” to people. Standing up for what is right doesn’t need to be rude or disrespectful.

I know what it feels like to work with “Wolf of Wallstreet” guys years ago. It felt like a rollercoaster and I saw a lot of people being disrespectful to others.

Respect is earned through character, consistency, and courage, not unanimous approval.

Build a reputation that reflects your values, even if it costs you a few opinions along the way.

When some people labeled me as “nice” they were surprised when I had courage to  speak up in the defense of others. This never compromised my positive approach. 

I found that people gravitated towards authenticity. 

BETTER TO BE A WOLF THAT 
SOME PEOPLE HATE, THAN
A DONKEY THAT EVERYONE RIDES.

Because in business, leadership, and life, authenticity will always outlast approval.

- Rachel Mitchell
```
