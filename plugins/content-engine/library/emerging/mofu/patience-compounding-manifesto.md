# Patience compounding manifesto

- **Archetype**: proof-backed-manifesto
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — sells a worldview, proves it with one number, hands off to a lead magnet.
- **Stage mechanic**: Sibling of proof-backed-permission-manifesto (same author, same week, same close): bold-promise hook → the one deciding variable → contrast the crowd vs. the patient few → the $15M proof line → effort-stats bridge into a free field manual. Welsh ran this skeleton three times in five days and all three cleared 4,700 reactions — the format, not the topic, is the engine.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan, second pass — author-seeded; three same-skeleton variants)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_youre-2-3-years-away-from-an-insane-life-activity-7483125306711724032-wzvo — Justin Welsh, 8,349 reactions / 1,345 comments, 2026-07-15 (scanned 2026-07-16)

  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_rich-is-visible-wealthy-is-invisible-activity-7482762919823314944-YlHL — Justin Welsh, 4,750 reactions / 1,466 comments, 2026-07-14 (variant: contrast-aphorism open, rich vs. wealthy)
- **Hook pattern**: "{Bold near-future promise}.\n\nOne question decides if you get it:" — or the contrast variant "{X} is visible.\n\n{Y} is invisible."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Bold-promise or contrast hook, two lines
  2. The single deciding variable, named plainly
  3. Rule stated as imperatives (decide / give / don't negotiate)
  4. Preempt the hard part ("it will feel slow") — the messy middle
  5. Flatter-the-reader turn ("mostly aren't better than you")
  6. The guardrail (patience about the *right* things)
  7. Proof line: $15M + years spent
  8. Effort-stats bridge (7 years, 250+ essays, voice notes)
  9. Anti-hype disclaimer ("zero hustle bro BS") + free-link CTA
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 1–2 line paragraphs, triads of imperatives, one number carrying all credibility. ~230 words.
- **Why it works**: Promise → obstacle → permission → proof is a complete emotional arc before the offer appears; the anti-hype line disarms exactly the skepticism the bold hook created.
- **Adaptation notes**: Needs a real proof number from the client's Proof section and a real lead magnet. The deciding variable should be one the client can defend in comments.
- **Watch-outs**: Running the skeleton multiple times a week only works with an audience that expects it — for clients, rotate. Bold promise without the guardrail block reads as hustle-bro.

## Top example — full post text

```
You're 2-3 years away from an insane life.

One question decides if you get it:

Do you have the patience?

Nearly every successful person I know decided, in advance, to do something big with their life.

Then they gave the decision decades to pay off instead of months.

Decide what you want. 
Give yourself a real window. 
Don't negotiate every time it feels slow.

Because it will feel slow. A lot.

There will be a stretch where nothing works.
Where quitting sounds great.
Where the numbers suck.

That stretch is not failure.

That's the messy middle, and it's the most crowded place in the world.

The people living their wildest lives mostly aren't better than you. 

They just refused to stop before the compounding kicked in.

But patience only compounds if you're patient about the right things. 

Spend a decade on the wrong work, and you just end up ten years older.

I spent seven years figuring out which things were worth the patience. 

Then I spent the last year turning it into something you can read in 20 minutes.

7 years of journaling and interviews and hours of voice notes distilled it into a 30-page field manual on how I built a $15M business without destroying my life.

There is zero hustle bro BS. No big "tricks." 

Just the 7 things actually worth figuring out.

Free here: https://buff.ly/qyPzps4
```
