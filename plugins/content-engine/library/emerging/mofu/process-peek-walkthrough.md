# Process-peek walkthrough

- **Archetype**: framework-walkthrough
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: "Peek inside my process" reframes a service pitch as generosity. The staged walkthrough (self-awareness → gaps → priorities → data → integration) proves the practitioner thinks in systems, which is the trust the ICP is buying.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan; rolls into Virio next Saturday)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/thejasonramsey_b2bmarketing-marketingstrategy-brandstrategy-activity-7481036246602055680-8Kqp — Jason W. Ramsey, 409 reactions / 46 comments, 2026-07-09 (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "Ever wonder how a {role} thinks when {job-to-be-done} — here is a peek inside my process"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Curiosity hook (peek inside)
  2. Stage 1 with its diagnostic questions
  3. Stages 2–4, each: label line + 2–3 action lines
  4. Integration close — the promised outcome state
- **Rhythm & formatting**: No punctuation at line ends, stanza-like stages, zero links. ~120 words — notably short for MOFU.
- **Why it works**: Sequenced stages imply a proprietary method without naming one. Diagnostic questions make the reader self-audit — the post does discovery on them.
- **Adaptation notes**: Swap the stages for the client's actual method (Canon only). Keep the stage-label + actions cadence; 4–5 stages max.
- **Watch-outs**: Generic stages ("understand, plan, execute") read as brochure. Each stage needs one word or question only a practitioner would use.

## Top example — full post text

```
Ever wonder how a B2B marketing strategist thinks when building a brand
Here is a peek inside my process

It starts with self-awareness
Where is the brand in the Self-Awareness Quadrants
Do they understand themselves
Do their customers understand their value

Next, I uncover the gaps
Audit messaging, visuals, and touchpoints
The gaps reveal the opportunities for clarity and trust

Then I prioritize action
Which quadrant needs immediate attention
Which messages need simplification
Which experiences need alignment

Data informs the next step:
Track audience response to campaigns
Test messaging, iterate visuals, refine positioning
Close the self-awareness gaps efficiently

Finally, integrate it all
Strategy, messaging, visuals, campaigns working together
Consistent clarity across every touchpoint
This is how B2B brands become recognized, trusted, and memorable
```
