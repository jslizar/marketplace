# Curiosity-gap newsletter teaser

- **Archetype**: curiosity-gap-teaser
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — converts feed attention into newsletter readership by withholding the payoff.
- **Stage mechanic**: Raises a threat every reader feels (your skills are commoditizing), introduces a specific unnamed counter-example ("a guy in a town of 158 people"), describes the shape of his advantage without naming it, and sends the reader to the essay for the answer. 431 comments on 624 reactions — the gap makes people talk.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan, second pass — author-seeded)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_your-skills-are-losing-value-fast-the-activity-7481701334002417664-UGJB — Justin Welsh, 624 reactions / 431 comments, 2026-07-11 (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "Your {asset} is losing value.\n\nFast."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Two-line threat hook
  2. Make it personal (the 20 years you spent mastering it)
  3. The era-ending claim (competence was the game; the game is ending)
  4. Pivot: "But there is one thing that cannot be copied."
  5. The mystery case study — hyper-specific details, advantage withheld
  6. Failed-copycats list (products / format / events — nothing works)
  7. Tease the simplicity ("sounds like a joke when you say it out loud")
  8. Personal stakes ("made me rethink what I'm building")
  9. CTA to the essay — no link in body, "read it below"
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Short declaratives, one-word paragraph ("Fast."), the copycat triad stacked. ~180 words.
- **Why it works**: Specificity (a town of 158 people) makes the withheld answer feel real rather than clickbait; the comment section becomes a guessing game, which feeds distribution.
- **Watch-outs**: The withheld answer must actually deliver in the essay or trust burns. One mystery per post — stacking gaps reads as bait.

## Top example — full post text

```
Your skills are losing value.

Fast.

The stuff that took you twenty years to master?

The writing, the systems, the funnels...

Those can now be produced by anyone with the right tools and an afternoon.

Competence used to be the whole game. 

Execute well, market well, and you'd win. 

That game is ending faster than most people want to admit.

But there is one thing that cannot be copied. 

I watched a guy in a town of 158 people build something around it that his competitors cannot touch, no matter how much they try and steal his playbook.

They copy the products. 
They copy the format. 
They copy the events. 

Nothing works.

His unfair advantage is so simple it sounds like a joke when you say it out loud. 

It took me a few minutes of knowing him to see it, and it made me rethink what I am building for the entire second half of my life.

I wrote about it this week.

Including the question I now run every project through before I touch it.

If you're wondering which of your skills still matter in five years, don't skip this issue.

Read it below now.
```
