# Proof-backed permission manifesto

- **Archetype**: proof-backed-manifesto
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: Grants the reader permission they crave ("you don't need anyone's approval"), then earns the right to say it with hard proof ($15M despite the doubters) and hands off into a free-guide lead magnet. Manifesto reach with lead-gen mechanics.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (this week's scan; rolls into Virio next Saturday)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/justinwelsh_you-dont-need-anyones-approval-stop-looking-activity-7482400527641710592-p8xU — Justin Welsh, 8,032 reactions / 1,581 comments, 2026-07-13 (scanned 2026-07-16)
- **Hook pattern**: "You don't need {permission everyone seeks}. Stop {seeking behavior}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Permission hook, two short lines
  2. Imperative burst (take / walk / have)
  3. Future-self justification ("when you wake up at 45")
  4. Personal receipts list — moves that drew disapproval
  5. The proof line (revenue number + doubters moved on)
  6. Bridge: "step one… step two" pivot to the resource
  7. Lead-magnet CTA (free guide + effort stats + link)
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 1–2 line paragraphs, imperatives stacked, one number doing all the proof work. ~220 words.
- **Why it works**: Emotional resonance first, credibility second, offer last — by the CTA the reader is already nodding. The effort stats (250+ essays, 10 interviews) make free feel expensive.
- **Adaptation notes**: The proof number must come from the client's Proof section — no number, no format. The lead magnet must genuinely exist.
- **Watch-outs**: Permission talk without receipts is a motivational poster. If the CTA arrives before the proof line, it reads as a funnel, not a manifesto.

## Top example — full post text

```
You don't need anyone's approval.

Stop looking for it.

Take the job you want.
Walk out of rooms that suck.
Have ambition and go get yours.

When you wake up at 45, you'll be thrilled you did.

Because the truth is, you've got to live for yourself and your family.

Be the person you most want to be...for them.

Don't worry about impressing anyone else.
Don't edit yourself for people who don't care.

All of my best moves came with disapproval.

I walked away from a great career.
Shared my opinions online every day.
Charged money for what I know. 
Built alone when everyone said I couldn't.

People had thoughts about all of it. 

Don't they always?

The business crossed $15M anyway, and most of the people with the strongest opinions have moved on to having opinions about something else.

Needing less approval from people is step one. 

Knowing what to build once you stop asking permission is step two.

So I wrote down exactly how to make that choice.

I went through 250+ of my own essays, hours of voice notes, and 10 interviews with people who built 8-figure businesses, and distilled all of the learnings into a 30-page free guide.

My field manual on building a $15M business without wrecking your life to do it.

Read it here: https://buff.ly/qyPzps4
```
