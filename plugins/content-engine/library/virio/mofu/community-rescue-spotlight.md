# Community rescue spotlight

- **Archetype**: Advocate spotlights one struggling ICP member and mobilizes the network for them
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: Makes a systemic problem (broken job market, ageism) undeniable through one named human, while positioning the author as the ICP's fiercest advocate. Shares are framed as two-second acts of kindness, so the post travels on conscience.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "{Age/status}. {Devastating one-word situation}. {Second devastating line}. Not because {they failed}, but because {the systemic problem}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Staccato devastation hook (3 fragment lines)
  2. System indictment line (shifts blame from person to market)
  3. "Meet {name} 👋🏼" + their credential in one line
  4. The irony line (the professional everyone depends on, discarded)
  5. Their words, quoted verbatim
  6. What employers are missing: ⭐ list of their real qualifications
  7. What they're looking for + location/availability
  8. Dignity framing ("She doesn't want pity… she wants to work")
  9. Mobilization close ("A share takes two seconds. Do your thing, LinkedIn 👊🏼")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 180–250 words. Fragment lines up top, emoji-anchored lists in the middle. Direct quotes mandatory.
- **Why it works**: One name beats a thousand statistics. The author spends nothing and gains the ICP's trust as someone who fights for them — which is exactly the standing MOFU needs.
- **Adaptation notes**: For clients whose ICP is suffering something systemic (laid-off recruiters, squeezed agency owners). Requires the person's enthusiastic consent and real details. The client's product stays out of the post entirely — the standing IS the return.
- **Watch-outs**: Exploiting someone's hardship for engagement is the obvious failure — consent, dignity framing, and follow-through (actually helping) are non-negotiable. One per quarter at most.
