# Client words verbatim

- **Archetype**: A client's casual quote, relayed verbatim, then decoded into the transformation mechanism
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: The client's own words ("my daughter hugs me now") carry emotional proof no copywriting can fake; the author's decode ("he didn't get there by talking more") converts the story into the method — teaching the approach while proving it works.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn

- **Hook pattern**: "He said it casually like it was nothing. (But damn, it said everything.)"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Understated hook + parenthetical
  2. The verbatim quote, multi-line, with its human specifics intact
  3. The spillover proof (not just her — his wife, his friends, his team)
  4. The decode: "He didn't get those results by {obvious approach}… He got there by {the actual mechanism}"
  5. Staccato mechanism lines ("More grounded. More still. More in control.")
  6. Stakes fork: "Don't get this right and: -{cost list}. But when you do? -{payoff list}"
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 200–280 words. The quote block is sacred — keep its casual voice. Dash lists for the stakes fork.
- **Why it works**: Verbatim speech has texture polished copy lacks ("I had to play it cool, but damn, that hit"). The decode elevates it from anecdote to framework, and the stakes fork makes doing nothing feel expensive.
- **Adaptation notes**: Use a real client quote with permission, kept verbatim including filler words. The mechanism decode must match the client's actual method. B2B version: the Slack message a customer sent after the metric moved.
- **Watch-outs**: Polished quotes read as fabricated — resist editing. Anonymize consistently or get named consent. The stakes fork tips manipulative if the costs are inflated.
