# Tiny-win confession

- **Archetype**: Playful confession of a small, specific win → gated how-to
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: The tiny concrete number ($9 in, $4,635 out) is more believable than a big vague one — it demos the approach in miniature, then gates the replication guide. Evaluators self-select by commenting.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/cas-feijen-66634426b_mom-i-did-it-i-made-a-ugc-creator-hold-activity-7269735561567899649-NwEI — Cas Feijen, 876 reactions / 3,047 comments, 2024-12-03
  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "{Playful exclamation}… I {tiny specific act} for {small cost} (it made me {larger return})"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Playful confession hook with cost-vs-return parenthetical
  2. The reveal/twist line ("and the video below. Is FULLY AI")
  3. Problem context: the known blocker this sidesteps, one line + arrow resolution
  4. Effort stat ("takes less than 1 minute", "saving me thousands every month")
  5. Gate: "I put together a full guide — want me to DM it for free?"
  6. Mechanics: like + comment "{KEYWORD}" (+ must-be-connected note)
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Under 100 words. Short lines, parentheticals doing the proof work. Casual register, one emoji max.
- **Why it works**: Small-stakes honesty reads as truth; the exact odd numbers ($9, $4,635) do the credibility lifting. The demo artifact (video/screenshot) attached to the post is the real proof — the text just frames it.
- **Adaptation notes**: Needs a real micro-win with real numbers and a real attached artifact. Works for any "cheap trick that beats the expensive way" story in the ICP's workflow. Keep the register playful; formal voice kills it.
- **Watch-outs**: Without the attached demo artifact the claims float. Inflated numbers get audited in comments. Don't stack multiple wins — one tiny win, told completely.
