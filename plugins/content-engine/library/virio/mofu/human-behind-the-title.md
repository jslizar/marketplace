# Human behind the title

- **Archetype**: Role-identity flip ("I'm {name} who happens to be {title}") + odd personal specifics
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: For personal-brand-led pipelines, buyers evaluate the person as much as the product. The flip thesis plus disarmingly specific quirks (fried-egg muffins, crying at fiction) converts a headline into a human evaluators feel they know — trust before the first call.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/isobel-cowell_hi-im-izzy-and-i-happen-to-be-a-head-of-activity-7031177837374033920-aGaZ — Isobel Cowell, 2,934 reactions / 228 comments, 2023-02-14
  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "Hi, I'm {name} and I happen to be a {title}. I am NOT a {title} who just happens to be called {name}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. The flip hook (two mirrored sentences, NOT in caps)
  2. The thesis: titles hide people
  3. "So yes, I spend 40 hours a week {job specifics}, but I also:" — emoji list of 4 oddly specific personal facts
  4. The flip restated as an aphorism ("I'm a human with a job. Not a job title that has a human attached.")
  5. Soft close ("And that's a good thing 🤍")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Under 150 words. The personal facts must be ODD-specific (fried-egg english muffins, not "coffee lover"). One heart-register emoji at close.
- **Why it works**: Specificity again — generic hobbies are invisible; weird true details are unforgettable. The mirrored flip is quotable, and the job-specifics line proves competence before the humanity play.
- **Adaptation notes**: For founders/executives whose personal brand carries the client's pipeline. Interview for the four REAL odd details — never invent them. Job-specifics line should name real weekly grind.
- **Watch-outs**: Cute details without the competence line reads as unserious. This format builds affinity, not authority — pair it in the calendar with proof-heavy posts, never run it standalone as the whole strategy.

## Top example — full post text

Hi, I'm Izzy and I happen to be a Head of Social.

I am NOT a Head of Social who just happens to be called Izzy.

We put so much value on someone's job title that we forget that there are real people behind every single LinkedIn headline.

So yes, I spend 40 hours a week editing content, running 1-2-1's and stressing over TikTok updates, but I also:

🍳 Have 2 fried-egg english muffins every morning
📞 Phone my mum whenever I'm walking alone
📚 Cry at every fiction book I read (sunglasses are my saviour when I'm on the train)
👩‍💼 Change my life ambitions depending on TV shows (grateful Suits wasn't the last show I watched before uni)

I'm a human with a job.

Not a job title that has a human attached to it.

And that's a good thing 🤍
