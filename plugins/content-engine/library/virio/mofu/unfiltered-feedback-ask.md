# Unfiltered feedback ask

- **Archetype**: Insider invites raw feedback on the thing they build, with an incentive
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: Asking "what do you HATE about {thing}?" turns the ICP's frustrations into a comment goldmine — market research, engagement, and "they actually listen" credibility in one post. Every comment is a pain point the client can build content (or product) against.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/sugju-choi_hey-linkedin-im-linkedins-one-of-the-first-activity-7419070510862319616-tSUL — Sugi Choi, 1,627 reactions / 960 comments, 2026-01-19
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/laurensurman_as-a-candidate-can-you-help-me-out-last-activity-7269695949705252864-n-lB — Lauren Surman, 336 reactions / 574 comments, 2024-12-03
- **Hook pattern**: "Hey {audience}! I'm {insider credential} — and I want your UNFILTERED feedback. What do you HATE the most about {the thing}?"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Direct-address hook: credential + permission-to-vent question
  2. Audience enumeration ("Whether you're a: > recruiter > job seeker > creator…") — everyone finds their line
  3. Re-focus line: don't care WHY you're here, tell me WHAT frustrates you
  4. Commitment: "I'll read every single one — even the spicy ones"
  5. Incentive with a real number ("6 months of Premium, $180 worth, for the best suggestion")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Under 120 words. Caps for emphasis (UNFILTERED, HATE, WHAT). Angle-bracket or arrow list for the audience enumeration. One emoji max.
- **Why it works**: Licensed venting is irresistible; the insider credential makes commenting feel like it might actually change something. The incentive is small but concrete — it signals seriousness, not desperation.
- **Adaptation notes**: The asker needs real standing over the thing being criticized (founder, PM, owner). Scope the "thing" to what the client can actually act on. The commitment line must be honored — reply activity is half the format.
- **Watch-outs**: Asking about a thing you can't change reads as farming. Defensive replies to spicy comments undo the whole play. Without the credential, this is just a poll.

## Top example — full post text

Hey LinkedIn! I'm LinkedIn's one of the first ever Product Builder - and I want your UNFILTERED feedback.

What do you HATE the most about LinkedIn?

Whether you're a:
> recruiter or a founder looking for the right talent
> job seeker hunting for opportunities
> creator building your personal brand
> developer sharing your work
> or just here to network

Doesn't matter WHY you're here, but I wanna know WHAT frustrates you the most .

Leave a comment below, I'll read every single one - even the spicy ones.

Bonus - I will give 6 month of FREE LinkedIn premium ($180 worth) to the best suggestion! 💗
