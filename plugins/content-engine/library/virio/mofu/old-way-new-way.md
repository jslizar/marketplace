# Old way / new way

- **Archetype**: "While most {ICP} were… we built…" before/after with quantified results
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: The bullet-vs-arrow contrast makes the status quo look painful line by line, then the results block makes the new way undeniable. The soft gate captures evaluators without a sales page.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "Never {struggle with the ICP's chronic pain} again"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Promise hook naming the chronic pain
  2. Old way: "While most {ICP} were:" + bullet list (•) of 4 pains
  3. New way: "We built {thing} that:" + arrow list (→) of 4 capabilities
  4. "The results?" + bullet list of 2–3 quantified outcomes
  5. Provocative one-liner ("Traditional {category} is dead.")
  6. Humanizing caveat ("It's not making decisions for our users…")
  7. Soft gate: numbered micro-steps ("1. Connect with me 2. Comment '{KEYWORD}'")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Bullets (•) for the old way, arrows (→) for the new — the visual switch IS the argument. 120–160 words. Percent stats in the results block.
- **Why it works**: Contrast is the fastest credibility structure on the feed; readers see their own workflow in the "old way" list and self-diagnose. The caveat line inoculates against the too-good-to-be-true reflex.
- **Adaptation notes**: Pains must be the ICP's real Tuesday-afternoon complaints, not strawmen. Results come from the client's Proof section only. Keep the bullet/arrow switch exactly.
- **Watch-outs**: Unbacked percentages ("98.3%") invite audits — round or source them. The "X is dead" line polarizes; drop it for conservative ICPs. Without the caveat block this reads as a pure ad and dies.
