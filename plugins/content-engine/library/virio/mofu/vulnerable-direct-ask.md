# Vulnerable direct ask

- **Archetype**: Stakes-on-the-table confession + a direct, specific ask to a named audience
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: Radical transparency about what's at stake ("825+ applications", "my savings are gone") earns the right to make a blunt ask. Addressed to a named audience segment ("if you are a hiring manager, this message is for you"), it converts sympathy into targeted action.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

- **Hook pattern**: "I'm writing this post as a final, last ditch effort. After {quantified attempts}, this is the only thing I haven't tried."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Last-resort framing + the quantified attempts
  2. Pre-empting advice: "I don't need new strategies" + the list of everything already tried
  3. The named-audience turn: "If you are {segment}, this message is for you"
  4. The confident core: what I bring, with concrete capabilities (not adjectives)
  5. The honest cost of the situation (confidence, false hope)
  6. The ask, stated once, plainly
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 200–300 words. Plain prose, no lists in the emotional sections. The tried-everything list is the credibility block.
- **Why it works**: The pre-emption block ("I've tried it") kills the advice-comments and forces the reader toward the only useful response: the ask. Vulnerability with receipts reads as courage, not weakness.
- **Adaptation notes**: For clients: adapt to business asks — a founder's "we need 10 design partners by March, here's exactly what we've tried" or an open call for a hard-to-fill role. The quantified attempts and the named audience are the format's load-bearing walls.
- **Watch-outs**: Run at most once — a second last-ditch post destroys the first. Self-pity without the confident-core block repels the exact audience it targets. For client brands, calibrate: desperation reads differently from a company than a person.

## Top example — full post text

I'm writing this post as a final, last ditch effort. After 8 months and 825+ applications, this is the only thing I haven't tried. This is my cry for help. My life as I know it is ending. My savings that I was responsible to have are gone. Everything I've built is falling apart and I am very close to losing everything.

I don't need new strategies. I've changed resumes, messaged recruiters, had direct referrals, contacted headhunters, tried roles within my network, outside. If you can think of it, I've tried it. There's no "strategy" that will work for me.

If you are a hiring manager or a direct recruiter this message is for you. I'm confident enough to know what I can bring. You will not find a harder worker, and I have results to prove it. I bring innovative solution, creative ideas, and stability to teams in need, as a Scrum Master or a Project Manager. I have a background in software engineering, so I can be a bridge between high ranking stakeholders and dev teams. I have a passion for finishing complex projects, many times multiple at a time, and seeing things through from start to finish.

Everything that has come close has just been false hope, and it has destroyed what confidence I still had in myself. Some may view this as "unprofessional" but the right organization for me would see this and be understanding. I didn't ask to be put in this position. I loved my job, and they unceremoniously dismissed me at a moments notice over a 1 way video call. I'm not sure what deity I pissed off but I can't get anything going for myself. I have valuable experience and a valuable education, I just need someone to realize that.

So here's what I'm asking for: connect me DIRECTLY with someone who is hiring for a job in my field, Scrum Master, Project Manager, or anything similar. I don't need links to job boards, I don't need links to roles with no human connection. If those worked, I wouldn't be writing this now. And no, I don't need "words of encouragement". I'm sorry if that comes across as rude or mean, but nice words will not pay my bills.

Thank you for taking the time to read this. I wish none of this had to be written. This is an extremely vulnerable post for me, but my life is at a tipping point and I can't wait any longer. Please help.
