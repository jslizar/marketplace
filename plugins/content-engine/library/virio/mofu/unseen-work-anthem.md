# Unseen work anthem

- **Archetype**: "Not everyone will see…" triplet → "but the right people will feel it" turn
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: Validates the ICP's specific invisible burdens, then resolves the ache with the "right people" turn. The ICP saves and reshares it as self-expression — the author becomes the voice of their unspoken experience.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "Not everyone will see {specific invisible labor}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. "Not everyone will see…" × 3, each naming a real, specific burden
  2. The turn, styled for emphasis: "But the right people will feel it."
  3. Who the right people are: ✨ list of 3 (each a relationship the ICP treasures)
  4. The reframe aphorism ("{Field} isn't about recognition, it's about impact")
  5. Quiet encouragement close + community hashtags
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Under 130 words. Anaphora (repeated openers) is the engine. Unicode bold/italic on the turn line. Soft emoji register.
- **Why it works**: Feeling seen is the strongest save-trigger for burnt-out professionals. The triplet must be so specific it could only come from someone who's lived it — that specificity IS the author's credential.
- **Adaptation notes**: Rewrite the three burdens from the ICP's actual week (for CISOs: the breach that didn't happen; for CS leads: the churn save nobody logs). Keep the anaphora structure and the single styled turn line.
- **Watch-outs**: Generic burdens turn it into inspirational wallpaper. More than 3 repetitions drags. This earns affinity, not proof — pair with evidence formats in the same calendar.
