# "Of course" listicle

- **Archetype**: "I'm a {role}. Of course I {insider truth}" anaphora + soft community invite
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: Riffs on a running meme format with insider truths only real practitioners know — each "of course" line is a badge check. The ICP comments with their own additions, and the close converts the laugh into a follow/join.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/patrice-dussault-translator-writer_litranslators-interpreters-activity-7323317989376679936-9H0R — Patrice Dussault, 512 reactions / 92 comments, 2025-04-30
  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "I'm a {role}. Of course, I'll {insider truth #1}"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Four "I'm a {role}. Of course, I {truth}" lines — escalating from relatable to only-us
  2. Separator (—)
  3. Soft pivot: "Hi! I'm {name}. Check out my profile and join my {n}+ community for more on {topics}"
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Under 90 words. Identical sentence scaffold on every line — the repetition IS the joke. One separator, one CTA line, done.
- **Why it works**: Meme-format familiarity lowers the guard; insider specificity ("find mistakes in Netflix subtitles") raises the hand. Comments become a collection of the ICP's own confessions — free market research.
- **Adaptation notes**: The four truths need real insider texture (for SDRs: "of course I have a Chrome window with 47 tabs of one prospect"). Ride the meme while it's warm; retire it when the feed saturates.
- **Watch-outs**: Truths any outsider could write kill the badge-check effect. More than 5 lines overstays. Meme formats date fast — this spec rotates out quicker than others; the refresh task should watch its example freshness aggressively.
