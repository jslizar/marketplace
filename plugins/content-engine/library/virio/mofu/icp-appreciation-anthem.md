# ICP appreciation anthem

- **Archetype**: Direct-address thank-you cataloguing the ICP's invisible work
- **Funnel stage**: MOFU — makes the problem urgent and the approach credible.
- **Stage mechanic**: Names the specific unseen labor the ICP performs (screening late, building careers, absorbing culture work) — proving the author understands their days, which is the standing MOFU runs on. The ICP tags colleagues, spreading it through exactly the right audience.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "To the {ICP} who:"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. "To the {ICP} who:" hook
  2. Emoji-anchored list of 4 specific invisible labors (each one verifiable ICP reality)
  3. "THANK YOU." — alone on its line
  4. One-line gratitude expansion
  5. Belonging line ("I couldn't be more grateful to be in this field with you")
  6. Occasion peg (the awareness day / season) + hashtags
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Under 120 words. The list is the post. All-caps THANK YOU is the pivot. Occasion-pegged when possible.
- **Why it works**: Specificity separates real appreciation from engagement bait — "stay late screening applicants" proves insider knowledge that "work so hard" never could. Peer-to-peer register ("in this field WITH you") beats vendor-to-buyer.
- **Adaptation notes**: The four labors must come from actual ICP research (support tickets, community threads, sales-call complaints). Peg to the ICP's real awareness day or season (close week, audit season, launch crunch).
- **Watch-outs**: Generic labors expose the post as pandering. A vendor thanking their buyers reads as sucking up unless the author has practitioner history — check the client's bio supports the "with you" claim.

## Top example — full post text

To the HR professionals who:

✊🏾 Stay at the office late screening job applicants.
✊🏾 Invest their time and energy into building employees' careers.
✊🏾 Pour their hearts into training programs around mental health.
✊🏾 Maintain a positive attitude to inspire a healthy workplace culture.

THANK YOU.

Thank you for your commitment toward improving our workplaces.

I couldn't be more grateful to be in this field with you.

Wishing you all a happy International Human Resources Day (or, as we like to call it, SHRM Day)!
