# Rivalry flip story

- **Archetype**: a named rival/copycat moment turned into an unexpected collab or win, told as a beat-by-beat story
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU
- **ABM mechanic**: both named companies come out heroes — the story flatters the rival's wit and the author's class. Prime reshare material for both orgs and business media.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task; pinned entries never auto-replaced)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jameswatt-investor-entrepreuner-punk_in-2020-aldi-infamously-copied-our-flagship-activity-7299734312311025664-XmXn — James Watt, 6,303 reactions / 450 comments, Feb 2025 (BrewDog × ALDI "YALDI IPA")
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/alex-tachalova_tell-me-youre-a-marketer-without-telling-activity-7323686235124752384-hNlh — Alexandra Tachalova, 6,933 reactions, May 2025 (Semrush 12-person era origin story)
- **Hook pattern**: "In {year}, {company} infamously {provocation}." — then the beats
- **Skeleton**:
  1. Date-stamped provocation hook naming the rival
  2. The provocation's audacity in 2–3 staccato lines ("Same branding. Same description. No shame.")
  3. "The normal response would be {lawyers/war}. Instead, we {unexpected move}:"
  4. Beat-by-beat escalation (the tweet, the reply, the counter) — real quotes
  5. The flip: the resolution that made both sides winners
  6. Principle close ("partners are always more valuable than rivals")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 200–350 words; one beat per line; real quoted exchanges; the principle stated once, not preached.
- **Why it works**: genuine narrative tension (rare on LinkedIn), both-sides flattery, and a quotable moral. Story does the selling.
- **Adaptation notes**: needs a real documented exchange — DMs, tweets, public replies. Works for copycat moments, poaching attempts, competitive dunks turned friendly.
- **Watch-outs**: never invent or embellish quotes; if the rival came out looking bad, this is a different (riskier) format — the flip must be real.

## Top example — full post text
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/jameswatt-investor-entrepreuner-punk_in-2020-aldi-infamously-copied-our-flagship-activity-7299734312311025664-XmXn (James Watt, 6,303 reactions)

```
In 2020, ALDI infamously copied our flagship beer.

ALDI - famous for its "inspired by" products - launched "Anti-Establishment IPA", a blatant copy of Punk IPA.

Same branding.
Same product description.
No shame 😅.

The normal response here would be to enter a legal battle, spending millions on fees to try and win a war.

Instead, we sent a tweet:

👉 "I wonder what ALDI would call Elvis Juice?"

Then, we took it a step further—announcing a new BrewDog beer called YALDI IPA.

Our message? Let's have some fun.

ALDI fired back: "We would have gone with ALD IPA. Send us a crate and we'll talk?"

So we did. We even redesigned the label to match their suggestion.

Within days, what could have been a corporate war turned into the most unexpected beer collab of 2020.

ALD IPA hit ALDI shelves across the UK and Germany.

When competition gets messy, don't default to lawsuits. Default to creativity.

Because sometimes, the best way to beat the game—is to play along.

And partners are always a lot more valuable than rivals.
```
