# Magic demo

- **Archetype**: teach a principle by showing how a named company made something "boring" spectacular
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU
- **Stage mechanic**: turns a named company into the hero case study of a teachable principle — their team reshares the flattery, prospects in similar businesses see themselves in it.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task; pinned entries never auto-replaced)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/moshe-pesach_wow-your-audeince-activity-7334232770568671233-xKer — Moshe Pesach, 7,861 reactions / 135 comments, May 2025 (Snowflake made data warehousing exciting)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/srgrace_ai-genai-llms-activity-7390394713264021505-YrId — Sourav Verma, 1,143 reactions, Nov 2025 (Cohere interview dialogue as a teaching device)
- **Hook pattern**: "Your \"boring\" {thing}? It's pure magic. [watch/look at this {unexpected analog}]"
- **Skeleton**:
  1. Reframe hook (boring → magic) + an unexpected analog (a physics teacher, a street vendor)
  2. The analog's trick in two lines (show, don't explain)
  3. Bridge: "Your {ICP's thing}? Same story."
  4. Numbered how-to (3 steps, each with 2–3 sub-bullets)
  5. The named-company proof: "Look at {company}:" — what they did, concrete numbers, and the outcome ("biggest software IPO in history")
  6. Challenge close: tell the reader to try it on their most boring feature
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 200–350 words; very short lines; numbered steps with sub-bullets; the named-company block carries the density.
- **Why it works**: the analog earns attention, the how-to earns saves, the named company earns credibility — three share triggers in one post.
- **ABM payoff**: the hero company's team reshares the flattering case study, earning the client the target's attention plus a proof asset to reference in the meeting.
- **Adaptation notes**: pick a hero company your ICP admires (or a target account with a genuinely great motion). Keep: reframe hook, analog, bridge, steps, named proof, challenge.
- **Watch-outs**: the hero company's numbers must be verifiable; without the analog this is a generic listicle.

```
Your "boring" solution? It's pure magic.
[Watch this Physics teacher]

That physics teacher just made basic principles fascinating.

How?

Not by explaining the theory.
By showing how it works.

Your B2B solution?
It's the same story.

You think: "It's too technical.".
They think: "Show me the magic."

Here's how to WOW your audience:

1. Break It Down
- Show the process
- Reveal the mechanics
- Demonstrate each step

2. Make It Real
- Live demonstrations
- Behind-the-scenes
- Real results

3. Show the Magic
- Highlight unique approaches
- Share insider tricks
- Reveal the "how"

Look at Snowflake:
Made data warehousing exciting
(yes, data warehousing!)

Instead of: "Enterprise data solution"

They showed:
- How they process queries 200x faster
- Live data sharing between companies
- Zero maintenance required

Result?
Tech leaders got obsessed with a "boring" database.

Biggest software IPO in history.

Your challenge:
Pick your most "boring" feature.
Break down how it works.
Make someone say, "Wow."

Remember:
To the right audience, HOW you do it is magic.
```
