# Insider leader vouch

- **Archetype**: Employee testifies to their own leadership's genius with witnessed specifics
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU — converts existing intent.
- **Stage mechanic**: An insider's firsthand detail ("can quote the income statement down to the decimal from 10 years ago") gives evaluators the view no analyst report offers: what the company is like from inside. Doubles as talent-pipeline proof ("p.s. we're hiring").
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/asharanchhod_wearecapitec-activity-7247086175050452992-dUxS — Asha Patel (Capitec CEO), 1,401 reactions / 138 comments, 2024-10-01
  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "One of the smartest and most successful {role}s of our time, {name}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Flat superlative hook, then straight into the witnessed scene ("While sitting in one of my very first meetings…")
  2. The impossible-to-fake detail (decimal-level recall, user-navigation fluency)
  3. The dual-ability frame (masters the detail AND casts the 10-year vision)
  4. The founding thesis and its vintage ("back in 2001… before those terms were in vogue")
  5. Receipts peg (yesterday's results as the thesis playing out)
  6. Gratitude close + culture hashtag + "p.s. we're hiring ;)"
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 150–220 words. Testimony register — scenes, not adjectives. The hiring P.S. stays lowercase-casual.
- **Why it works**: Employees vouching upward, unprompted, is the strongest culture signal on the feed. The witnessed specifics do what "great leadership" never can; buyers and candidates both read it as "this place is real."
- **Adaptation notes**: Works when a client's team members genuinely admire leadership — the author is the employee, not the company account. The detail must be firsthand-witnessed, never briefed.
- **Watch-outs**: If it reads as assigned homework, it damages both parties — voice must be the employee's own. This is about the author's OWN org; praising another company's exec to court them is the ABM engine's lane.

## Top example — full post text (excerpt)

One of the smartest and most successful CEOs of our time, Gerrie Fourie. While sitting in one of my very first meetings when joining Capitec to discuss our app, I was struck by Gerrie's detailed knowledge and insight on things like user navigation. He knows his entire business like the back of his hand, and amongst many things, can quote the income statement (down to the decimal) from 10 years ago.
At the same time, he has the unique ability to craft and inspire the organisation with his vision for the next 10 years.
As one of the founders, back in 2001, they crafted a business plan which set out to bring the fundamentals of Simplicity, Affordability, Accessibility and Personal Service to a complex Banking industry way before those terms were in vogue. If you caught yesterday's interim results - you would have seen how that vision continues to be brought to life through solutions that truly add value and remove friction in the lives of the clients we serve.
It really is quite remarkable, and it's been a privilege to be part of this team and learn from their genius.
#WeAreCapitec

and p.s we're hiring ;)
