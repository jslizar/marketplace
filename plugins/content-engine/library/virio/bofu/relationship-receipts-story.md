# Relationship-receipts story

- **Archetype**: Decades-spanning personal story proving what a partner is like over time
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU — converts existing intent.
- **Stage mechanic**: For businesses where trust IS the product (advisory, investing, agencies, fractional roles), a true long-arc story with small human details is the strongest close — it shows character across decades, which no case study can.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/crystal-lim-lange_leadership-activity-7245301455186788352-gKam — Crystal Lim-Lange, 1,930 reactions / 103 comments, 2024-09-27
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/josephdesena_one-minute-i-was-in-the-back-of-a-taxi-in-activity-7341068070196527105-8KyS — Joe De Sena (Spartan), 769 reactions / 94 comments, 2025-06-18
- **Hook pattern**: "In {year} I was {low moment}. {One vivid sentence of the scene.}"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Dated low-moment hook, told plainly
  2. The person who showed up: what they said/did, with one oddly specific detail (the washed fruit)
  3. Time jump: "Today, two decades later…" — where both parties landed
  4. The reunion scene: proof the character held ("some things don't change")
  5. The credential reveal: who this person turned out to be
  6. One-line moral ("Sometimes the good guys win")
  7. Engagement invitation: "share/tag someone who…"
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 200–260 words, warm narrative prose, one emoji tolerated at emotional peaks. Names tagged in full.
- **Why it works**: The oddly specific detail is the truth signal; the time jump is the proof structure. Readers tag their own "Vineet", which spreads it — and the named person's org gets a halo no ad buys.
- **Adaptation notes**: Must be true and the named person must consent. For client use: founders telling the story of a long-standing advisor, investor, or first customer. The specific detail cannot be manufactured — find the real one in the interview.
- **Watch-outs**: Sentimentality without the credential reveal is TOFU inspiration, not BOFU proof — the reveal is what makes it a buying signal. If the story's engine becomes glorifying the named person's COMPANY to win their business, that's the abm lane.

## Top example — full post text

In 2003 I was retrenched from an investment bank. I had to pack up my things in front of everyone, and I was in shock. This colleague of mine, Vineet Mishra pulled me aside, and he told me that he wasn't sad for me. He said that years from now I would look back and realise that there was something better for me out there.

Today 2 decades later, I'm my own boss with 3 successful companies, doing what I love every day. I still think of Vineet and how kind he was. He looked out for all of us junior analysts when we worked till 3 am in the morning and told us to chill. He nagged me about not eating and one time he even washed my office fruit for me and made me eat it to ward off scurvy.

Yesterday I had lunch with him and connected for the first time in 17 years. He told me I wasn't eating enough and tried to make me finish my chicken. Some things don't change 😂

It made me so proud and happy to hear that he was now a Managing Director running Investment Banking for the region at J.P. Morgan. It's rare to find someone in this industry who is as solid and kind as they are competent.

Sometimes the good guys win. 🥹

Thanks Vineet❤️
