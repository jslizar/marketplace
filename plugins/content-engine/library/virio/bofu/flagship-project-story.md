# Flagship project story

- **Archetype**: Agency narrates its biggest-ever project for a marquee client, then invites the next one
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU — converts existing intent.
- **Stage mechanic**: "Largest-ever" + a marquee client name + the craft philosophy ("converting positioning into presence") gives evaluating buyers all three purchase inputs at once: scale capability, brand trust, and method. The visit invitation makes the proof physically checkable.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/freeform_megatall-construction-planning-activity-7254740867205230593-lLHV — James Bowles (Freeform × Jeddah Tower), 5,952 reactions / 227 comments, 2024-10-22
- **Hook pattern**: "When {marquee client} set out to create {their superlative project}, {we} were entrusted to {the mission, in their brand's language}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Entrusted hook: client + superlative + mission
  2. Elevation line ("this wasn't just about X - it was about shaping how the world experiences it")
  3. The delivery: what was built, with the named sub-brands/details that prove intimacy
  4. The method paragraph ("Our strength lies in…") — the agency's transferable philosophy
  5. Full tag roll: every collaborator on both sides
  6. Experience invitation ("Flying into Mumbai soon? Step in") + next-buyer line
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 180–250 words. Client's brand vocabulary woven in (gold-and-barrel universe). Strong photos mandatory. Tag roll extends reach through both orgs.
- **Why it works**: The marquee client's trust is the headline; the method paragraph converts one project into a repeatable promise; the physical invitation ("step in and experience it") is proof no deck can match.
- **Adaptation notes**: For any services client's flagship delivery. The superlative must be true and specific ("largest-ever shop-in-shop at CSMIA Arrivals"). Client approval on framing and tags first.
- **Watch-outs**: ABM boundary: this converts the NEXT buyer using a past client as proof — if the aim is winning MORE work from the named client, run the abm lane's credit-roll instead. Overclaiming your role when partners shared the work gets corrected publicly.

## Top example — full post text

When Suntory Global Spirits set out to create its largest-ever shop-in-shop at Mumbai’s Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj International Airport (Arrivals), WHITE was entrusted to translate their gold-and-wooden barrel universe into an experience that global travellers won’t forget.

This wasn’t just about showcasing spirits - it was about shaping how the world experiences them. In partnership with Ospree Duty Free, we brought to life a first-of-its-kind, multi-brand concept that housed icons like Hibiki, Bowmore, Maker’s Mark, Roku, and Haku—all under one beautifully curated space.

At White, our strength lies in converting positioning into presence. Every design choice - material, print, texture, and prop - was deliberately crafted to echo Suntory Global Spirits’ legacy and modern luxury codes. The result? A sensorial journey that reflects the best of East and West, where storytelling and spatial design come together seamlessly.
