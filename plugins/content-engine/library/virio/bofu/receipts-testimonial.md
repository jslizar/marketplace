# Receipts testimonial

- **Archetype**: Customer testifies with the exact promise made, the promise beaten, and the numbers
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU — converts existing intent.
- **Stage mechanic**: The promise-vs-delivery structure ("said $30K in a day; got us $50K in 10 minutes") is the tightest proof loop on the feed. The responsiveness detail ("text him 24/7, reply in 5 minutes") answers the evaluator's other question: what's the service like after signing?
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nicoaferr_cyrus-shirazi-texted-me-a-few-months-ago-activity-7312878138370240515-IYuM — Nico F. (Haven / Default), 211 reactions / 20 comments, 2025-04-01
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/robertreffkin_thank-you-andy-florance-ceo-costar-group-activity-7352789119678251009-URoE — Robert Reffkin (Compass, on Homes.com), 480 reactions / 41 comments, 2025-07-20
- **Hook pattern**: "{Name} texted me a few months ago saying {vendor} could get {company} {specific promise}. They ended up getting us {bigger number} in {shorter time}…"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Promise hook, with names and the original claim
  2. The beat: delivery exceeded promise (bigger number, less time)
  3. The blind-spot confession ("I had no clue we were leaving tens of thousands on the table")
  4. The service detail (24/7 texting, 5-minute replies — oddly specific)
  5. Direct recommendation naming the exact buyer ("If you're a founder who wants to…")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Under 120 words. Numbers unrounded and odd ($50K, 10 minutes). Names tagged. No hashtags needed.
- **Why it works**: Beating a stated promise is more credible than any absolute claim — it implies the vendor underpromises. The confession block gives every reader the same fear ("what am I leaving on the table?"), and the recommendation tells them exactly who should act.
- **Adaptation notes**: This is the format the client's HAPPY CUSTOMERS post — the client's job is to earn it and (at most) prompt it. B2B mechanics: real promise, real delivery numbers, real texting story. Never draft it FOR the customer wholesale; source their actual words.
- **Watch-outs**: Round, suspiciously clean numbers ($50,000 exactly, every time) read as fabricated. If the client writes it and the "customer" posts it verbatim, discovery is reputational damage for both.
