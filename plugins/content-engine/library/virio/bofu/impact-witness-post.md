# Impact witness post

- **Archetype**: Third party spontaneously testifies to a company's real-world impact they encountered
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU — converts existing intent.
- **Stage mechanic**: An unprompted witness ("I saw this ambulance yesterday") with verified numbers converts a company's program into proof of character. For buyers choosing between similar vendors, demonstrated values break the tie. "This isn't just CSR. It's life-saving."
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/amithe-gamage_srilanka-1990suwaseriya-publicprivatepartnership-activity-7336225258124791808-kE9w — Amithe Gamage (WSO2 / Suwa Seriya), 1,840 reactions / 31 comments, 2025-06-05
  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "Thank you, {company}, and all who care. I recently saw {the concrete artifact} and it reminded me of the real impact {kind of support} can make."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Gratitude hook naming the company and the encounter
  2. Program context with real numbers (322 ambulances, 50 out of service, Rs. 525M contributed)
  3. The gap-and-response mechanic (the need, and who stepped up)
  4. The reframe line ("This isn't just CSR. It's life-saving.")
  5. Repeat thanks widened to all participants
  6. Provenance note ("Photo: I saw this ambulance yesterday, rushing to save a life")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 130–180 words. The photo with provenance is the credibility anchor. Numbers verified.
- **Why it works**: Third-party spontaneity is unpurchasable — the author has no stake, the encounter was accidental, the photo is timestamped reality. The company gets proof-of-values it could never post itself.
- **Adaptation notes**: Two uses — the client's champions writing about the client's program, or the client witnessing a partner/customer's impact (which that org will amplify). The encounter must be real; the numbers must check out.
- **Watch-outs**: If the "witness" turns out to be connected to the company, the whole post inverts into astroturf. Impact-washing a company with unrelated controversies invites the comment section to correct the record.

## Top example — full post text

Thank you, WSO2, and all who care.
I recently saw an ambulance adopted by WSO2 as part of Sri Lanka's 1990 Suwa Seriya emergency service, and it reminded me of the real impact corporate support can make.
As of 2025, Suwa Seriya (One of the world's most digitally advanced and free ambulance services in Sri Lanka, called '1990 Suwa Seriya) operates 322 ambulances, providing free emergency care islandwide. But due to funding and maintenance challenges, over 50 are currently out of service.
To bridge the gap, the "Adopt an Ambulance" initiative was launched. Private companies have already stepped up, adopting over 100 ambulances and contributing more than Rs. 525 million to keep them running.
This isn't just CSR. It's life-saving.
Thank you, WSO2, and all the organizations choosing to invest in something that touches real lives.
Photo: I saw this ambulance yesterday, rushing to save a life.
