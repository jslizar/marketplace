# Decade-later testimonial

- **Archetype**: Unsolicited endorsement proven by relationship longevity
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU — converts existing intent.
- **Stage mechanic**: A testimonial dated across a decade is unfakeable proof — the endorser shows the promise made at the start and the relationship still standing years later. For evaluators, it answers the real question: "what are these people like AFTER the deal closes?"
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

- **Hook pattern**: "When I met {person} over a decade ago when {original context}, he told me that {the promise/differentiator}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Origin-story hook: the meeting + the promise made back then
  2. The receipts: "Well, not only was {promise kept}, but {beyond-the-call specifics}"
  3. The present-day moment that prompted the post (a visit, a milestone)
  4. Direct recommendation to the reader's segment ("For those young entrepreneurs looking to…, there is none better")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 100–150 words, warm prose, first person. Names in full. No hashtags, no gate — the recommendation line is the CTA.
- **Why it works**: Time is the one proof element money can't buy. The endorser spends their own credibility, and the "even after I left" details prove the relationship outlived the transaction.
- **Adaptation notes**: This is a format the CLIENT'S CHAMPIONS post, or the client posts about a long-term partner — either direction works. Needs a real long relationship with datable specifics. The final recommendation must name the exact buyer segment.
- **Watch-outs**: Reads as staged if the endorser has an obvious current stake — the power is in the absence of motive. Recent relationships can't run this play; don't force it.

## Top example — full post text

When I met Amir Goldman over a decade ago when he was thinking of investing in Payoneer, he told me that Susquehanna Growth Equity (SGE) was different because they didn't have a time horizon that they had to exit an investment/relationship. Well, not only was SGE a great investor who is still invested in Payoneer, and Amir a great Board Member, but Amir has proactively maintained a personal relationship with me even after I left the firm.

It was an honor to host Amir when he stopped by Fireblocks to check in to see how I was doing. For those young entrepreneurs looking to bring on an investor that will stick with you and support you, there is none better than Amir and SGE.
