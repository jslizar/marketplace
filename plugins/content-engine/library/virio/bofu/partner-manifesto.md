# Partner manifesto

- **Archetype**: Sponsored partnership disclosed inside a personal philosophy the product happens to complete
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU — converts existing intent.
- **Stage mechanic**: Instead of "here's my discount code," the creator builds a worldview ("waiting for the perfect moment is the trap") and positions the partner as its natural conclusion. The single link CTA converts readers already nodding along.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "We tell ourselves we'll start when things 'settle down'..."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Universal self-deception hook (the excuse everyone recognizes)
  2. The two what-if questions that corner it
  3. The philosophy line ("Growth doesn't happen when life clears a path…")
  4. Partnership disclosure, woven in ("That's why I'm happy to partner with {brand}")
  5. Why THIS partner fits the philosophy (the product attribute that resolves the tension)
  6. The permission line ("the right moment is whenever you decide to start")
  7. Arrow CTA + single link + partner hashtags
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 150–220 words. Reflective register, second person plural drifting to second person. One link, one arrow CTA.
- **Why it works**: The philosophy does the selling before the product appears, so the disclosure lands as context, not interruption. Readers convert on the worldview, which the product merely operationalizes.
- **Adaptation notes**: For client partnership/ambassador programs. The philosophy must be one the creator already visibly holds — retrofitted worldviews read as scripts. Product attribute must genuinely resolve the tension named.
- **Watch-outs**: Disclosure is non-negotiable (legally and for trust) — bury it and the comments will find it. Too much philosophy with too little product specificity converts nobody.
