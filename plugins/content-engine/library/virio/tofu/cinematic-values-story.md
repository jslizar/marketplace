# Cinematic values story

- **Archetype**: Micro-story of ordinary people under pressure → values takeaway
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP; client-agnostic on the surface.
- **Stage mechanic**: A cold reader shares it because it moves them and says something about who THEY are. Zero brand required; the values (integrity, commitment, courage) map onto whatever the client stands for.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "In the middle of {crisis}, {ordinary people} chose not to {expected self-preserving act}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. One-line scene: crisis + the surprising choice
  2. Staccato escalation ("They chose to stay. / To protect. / To do the job…")
  3. Reframe line: "That's not just {profession-word}. That's {value} in its rawest form."
  4. Zoom-out reflection (2 lines, no applause/no spotlight framing)
  5. "Bottomline:" aphorism closer
  6. Hashtags (3–5, values-flavored)
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Very short lines, heavy line breaks, sentence fragments as beats. Under 120 words. No links, no CTA.
- **Why it works**: Vicarious moral elevation — readers share to signal their own values. The staccato rhythm makes it read in 15 seconds. The "Bottomline:" line gives commenters something quotable.
- **Adaptation notes**: Change the story and the value word to match the client's world (a nurse story for healthtech, a line-cook story for hospitality SaaS). Must be a TRUE, verifiable story. The skeleton's beat structure must not change.
- **Watch-outs**: Invented or unverifiable stories get called out in comments and crater trust. Tragedy-mining reads as exploitative — the story must end in dignity, not grief.

## Top example — full post text

In the middle of a violent earthquake, nurses in a childcare room chose not to run.

They chose to stay.
To protect.
To do the job they promised to do—even when death felt closer than safety.
That's not just professionalism.
That's integrity in its rawest form.

A reminder that real commitment shows itself when the stakes are highest.
No applause. No spotlight. Just courage.

They didn't just save lives. They showed us how to live.

Bottomline: When the ground trembles, character stands still.
