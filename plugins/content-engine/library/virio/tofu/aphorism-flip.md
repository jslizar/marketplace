# Aphorism flip

- **Archetype**: Contrarian two-beat aphorism + arrow-list of consequences
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP; client-agnostic on the surface.
- **Stage mechanic**: The flip ("Don't choose X. Choose Y.") compresses a whole worldview into a shareable rule. People repost it as advice they wish they'd been given.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/lindale20_dont-choose-a-job-choose-a-manager-a-activity-7263235531411501056-_JV6 — Linda Le, 62,028 reactions / 2,253 comments, 2024-11-15
  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "Don't choose {the obvious thing}. Choose {the overlooked thing}."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Two-beat flip hook
  2. One line deflating the obvious thing ("A job is a paycheck and a salary.")
  3. "But {the overlooked thing} determines:" + arrow list (↳) of 4–6 stakes
  4. One-line dark consequence of choosing wrong
  5. Aphoristic restatement closer tying to received wisdom ("People don't leave companies… they leave managers")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Under 100 words. Arrow bullets (↳). One idea per line. No links, no hashtags needed, no CTA.
- **Why it works**: The flip creates instant agreement-or-argument; the arrow list converts an opinion into apparent structure. Low effort to read, high status to repost.
- **Adaptation notes**: The flip must sit inside the ICP's daily decisions (for a devtools ICP: "Don't choose a framework. Choose a maintainer."). Stakes list must be things the ICP actually loses sleep over. Keep the two-beat hook exactly — lengthening it kills it.
- **Watch-outs**: A flip everyone already agrees with earns nothing — it needs a real opposing camp. Chaining multiple flips in one post dilutes to mush.

## Top example — full post text

Don't choose a job. Choose a manager.

A job is a paycheck and a salary.

But a manager determines :

↳ Your mental health
↳ Your promotions
↳ Your career growth
↳Your work-life balance
↳ Your best interest

A toxic manager will demote not only your career, but your physical, emotional, and mental health.

People don't leave companies, jobs, or cultures, they leave their managers because a manager determines everything from day 1 ❤️
