# Expert witness declaration

- **Archetype**: Credentialed expert declares what a stunning artifact means, in oracle cadence
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP; client-agnostic on the surface.
- **Stage mechanic**: An attached jaw-drop artifact (video/demo) + an expert saying "this is real, and here is what it means" — the expert's certainty is the shareable part. Ends with a quotable epigram people repost as prophecy.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/drjimfan_these-are-not-cgi-reinforcement-learning-activity-7277013250565709826-uqyV — Jim Fan, 16,032 reactions / 522 comments, 2024-12-23
  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "These are not {the dismissal everyone reaches for}. {The real explanation} is so back."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Preemptive debunk hook ("These are not CGI")
  2. The unifying insight, stated with total confidence (one mechanism explaining two domains)
  3. The epigram ("Give me a reward function, and I shall move the world.")
  4. The periodization couplet ("2024 is the year of X. 2025 is the year of Y.")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Under 90 words. Declarative sentences only. No hedging, no hashtags, no CTA. The artifact does the awe; the text does the meaning.
- **Why it works**: Certainty from a real expert is rare and magnetic. The epigram and the periodization couplet are pre-packaged quotes — readers steal them for their own posts and cite the author.
- **Adaptation notes**: Requires the client's genuine domain authority and a genuinely stunning artifact. The dismissal being debunked must be what skeptics actually say. Craft the epigram hard — it's the whole format.
- **Watch-outs**: Certainty without credentials reads as hype. If the artifact underwhelms, the confident register becomes embarrassing. Wrong prediction ages loudly — but memorably wrong beats forgettably safe.
