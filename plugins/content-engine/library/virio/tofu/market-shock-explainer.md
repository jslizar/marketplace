# Market-shock explainer

- **Archetype**: Breaking-news stat bomb + rapid-fire numbers thread explaining why it matters
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP; client-agnostic on the surface.
- **Stage mechanic**: Rides a live market shock the whole feed is refreshing about, and wins by being the clearest explainer with the starkest number pairs. Positions the author as the one to follow during chaos.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/stevenouri_ai-deepseek-innovation-activity-7289710857322708992-Y8iV — Steve Nouri (DeepSeek crash), 57,988 reactions / 3,720 comments, 2025-01-27
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/eddie-donmez-538b55b5_growth-markets-finance-share-7061643142193664001-dwe0 — Eddie Donmez (Saudi Aramco profits), 13,570 reactions / 700 comments, 2023-05-09
- **Hook pattern**: "{Actor} has now {jaw-drop consequence with $ figure}. {Second escalating figure}!"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Double stat-bomb hook (the damage, then the bigger damage)
  2. The David-vs-Goliath one-liner ("All because a $6M startup did what X claimed was impossible")
  3. "Here's why 🧵:" promise
  4. Stark number pairs, side by side (them: $600M / challenger: $6M — repeated across 2–3 dimensions)
  5. "This isn't just X — it's a paradigm shift" escalation
  6. Bullet recap of the challenger's edge
  7. Borrowed-authority quote (a named expert's framing)
  8. Open question CTA ("What's your take? 👇")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 200–280 words. Number pairs on adjacent lines carry the argument. Short declaratives between them.
- **Why it works**: Timing + arithmetic. The side-by-side numbers let readers feel the asymmetry themselves; the borrowed quote gives them a phrase to repeat in meetings.
- **Adaptation notes**: Only run within ~48h of a real shock in the ICP's market. All figures verified this run — this format lives and dies on the numbers being right.
- **Watch-outs**: Wrong or stale numbers get corrected loudly. Gloating over a company's loss reads badly if the client sells to that company's ecosystem.
