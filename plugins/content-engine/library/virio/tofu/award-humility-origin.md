# Award humility origin

- **Archetype**: Public honor accepted, then immediately converted into the messy origin story
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP; client-agnostic on the surface.
- **Stage mechanic**: The award earns the right to talk; the cringe-filled origin (deleted drafts, pep talks, obsessive checking) earns the relatability. Readers share the "there is no secret formula" takeaway as encouragement to their own network.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/melrobbins_time100creators-activity-7349156993175101442-wWA1 — Mel Robbins (TIME100), 31,811 reactions / 1,644 comments, 2025-07-10
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/mo-abudu_this-post-isnt-about-boasting-its-about-activity-7332703295074938880-xS7Y — Mo Abudu (EbonyLife Group), 13,330 reactions / 1,465 comments, 2025-05-26
- **Hook pattern**: "Thank you {institution} for {honor}!!! I am deeply honored!" → immediate reframe ("As a {unexpected identity}, I am also proud to be {the outlier angle}")
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Gratitude + the honor, named
  2. The outlier reframe (oldest on the list, "not in spite of my age but because of it")
  3. The thesis it proves ("it's never too late")
  4. Origin flashback: the unglamorous why ("just trying to pay our bills")
  5. The messy middle, in painful specifics (pep talks, deleting posts, hundreds of drafts)
  6. Direct address turn: "So here's my message to you:"
  7. The takeaway line ("There is no secret formula. It's you against you.")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 200–280 words. First person, spoken cadence, "Every. Single. Post." style emphasis beats.
- **Why it works**: Status + vulnerability in one post — the honor makes it news, the mess makes it shareable. The outlier reframe gives an underrepresented slice of the audience a flag to wave.
- **Adaptation notes**: Needs a real external honor (award, list, milestone) and a real messy middle with specifics only the client would know. The message-to-you turn must serve the reader, not the brand.
- **Watch-outs**: Skipping the mess makes it a humblebrag. Manufactured humility ("so blessed") reads instantly. Don't run twice a year — scarcity is part of the format.

## Top example — full post text

Thank you TIME for naming me as  one of the “Titans” on your first-ever TIME100 Creator List!!! I am deeply honored!

As a 56-year-old woman, I am also proud to be one of the oldest people on the list, not in spite of my age but because of it.

It proves what I say to you every week on The Mel Robbins Podcast: it’s never too late and you’re never too old to start creating the life you’ve always wanted.

When I started posting online back in 2012,  I wasn’t “trying” to go viral. I was just trying to book a few speaking gigs so I could pay our bills and get out of debt. That’s it. Just me, a married mother of 3, hoping someone would take me seriously and hire me.

In the beginning, every post felt cringey.

Every. Single. Post.

I’d spend hours crafting the “perfect” thing. I’d have to give myself a 10-minute pep talk before hitting “post,” and when I finally did it, I’d then check it obsessively. Five minutes would pass, and if the likes or comments weren’t what I was hoping for, my insecurity would kick in and I’d delete it.

I created hundreds of draft posts...and they would just sit there, staring back at me for years.

So here’s my message to you:

There is no secret formula.

It’s you against you. The only difference between you and most of us on the #TIME100Creators list is that, despite how cringey it felt in the beginning, we got started and kept going. Day after day. Year after year. And in my case, 13 years later, here I am…  And that’s how you’re going to change your life, too.

So start now. Even if you’re afraid of what people might think. 

In fact, just use The Let Them Theory.

If they roll their eyes? Let Them. 
If they judge you? Let Them.

You’re not creating for them...
You’re building something for YOU.

You don’t need to be perfect. You need to begin.

Your future self will thank you, and so will I.

And in case no one else tells you, as your friend, I want to tell you that I love you and I believe in you and your ability to create a better life. Forcing yourself to do the things that you don’t feel like doing is exactly how you do it. Xo Mel

And a huge shoutout to everyone on the list and who is inspired to be creating 💚
