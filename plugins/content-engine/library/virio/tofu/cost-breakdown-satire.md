# Cost-breakdown satire

- **Archetype**: Provocative first-person number breakdown with a straight face
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP; client-agnostic on the surface.
- **Stage mechanic**: The absurd-but-itemized math is comment bait — half the audience argues the numbers, half shares the joke. Reach comes from the fight in the comments.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.

  - (second example: to be filled by the scheduled refresh)
- **Hook pattern**: "I {enviable status stat} and it's utterly {complaint}. Let's break it down."
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Enviable-status hook + deadpan complaint
  2. "Let's break it down."
  3. Itemized line-item list, each with a real-looking number; one or two absurd items buried in the middle (the tell)
  4. Total line
  5. Deadpan conclusion restating the complaint with the numbers
  6. One-line rhetorical closer ("What is wrong with this city?")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: Line-item list, one per line, `label: $amount`. 100–150 words. No hashtags, no links, no CTA.
- **Why it works**: Rage-bait with plausible deniability — the buried absurd items ($1368 Cybertruck lease next to $20 shirt) let readers in on the joke while others take the bait. Specific numbers force engagement; nobody argues with vibes, everyone argues with math.
- **Adaptation notes**: Swap the domain to the ICP's world (a founder's "why our $2M seed is gone in 14 months", an ops leader's "why this 'free' tool costs us $400k"). The numbers must be internally consistent; the absurd items must be findable but not flagged.
- **Watch-outs**: Miss the satire register and it reads as genuine entitlement — the deadpan must be detectable. Never use real client financials.
