# Future-wonder demo

- **Archetype**: "What if?" reveal of an emerging technology, explained with everyday stakes
- **Funnel stage**: TOFU — earns reach with the ICP; client-agnostic on the surface.
- **Stage mechanic**: Pure curiosity content — shows the ICP a piece of the future they hadn't seen, in language their non-expert boss would understand. Positions the author as a scout worth following; the closing question invites practitioners to fill the comments.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: exactly 2, refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task from the viral_posts_all corpus.
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/philipp-kozin_smartglass-futureofliving-interiordesign-activity-7325600040566013953-wkKA — Philipp Kozin (smart glass), 4,778 reactions / 294 comments, 2025-05-06
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/harbech_disney-just-released-this-demo-they-activity-7154755444320743424-a2bi — Martin Harbech (Disney HoloTile), 17,178 reactions / 558 comments, 2024-01-21
- **Hook pattern**: "{Evocative rename of the tech}: {three-adjective promise}. What if {everyday object} could {magic behavior} at the touch of a button?"
- **Skeleton** (block sequence):
  1. Coined-name title line + what-if question
  2. "Meet {the tech}" — plain-language mechanism in 2 sentences
  3. Practical payoff list woven into prose (retrofit, fewer parts, better aesthetics)
  4. Zoom-out line: which industries this rewires
  5. The "perfect example of {two fields} merging" frame
  6. Practitioner question CTA ("Have you seen this in action or worked with it?")
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 120–170 words. Accessible register — no jargon without immediate translation. Demo video/image attached. Hashtag block tolerated at the end.
- **Why it works**: Wonder is the most shareable emotion after outrage, and much safer for a brand. The everyday framing ("your window blinds") makes deep tech land with a lay audience.
- **Adaptation notes**: Pick emerging tech adjacent to the client's category (close enough to signal expertise, far enough to not be a pitch). The mechanism sentences must be accurate — practitioners will check.
- **Watch-outs**: Overclaiming maturity ("this will replace X next year") draws expert correction. Without a visual, wonder doesn't transmit.

Smart Curtains of the Future: Transparent, Flexible, and Electric

What if your window blinds could disappear at the touch of a button?

Meet the next generation of "modern curtains" — ultra-thin, electrically conductive films that can be applied directly to glass surfaces. With a small voltage, they switch from transparent to opaque, offering instant privacy, glare control, and energy efficiency.

These smart films can be retrofitted onto existing windows, making them a game-changer for architecture, automotive, and interior design. Think fewer mechanical parts, better aesthetics, and smarter control.

A perfect example of how materials science and design are merging to redefine the spaces we live and work in.
