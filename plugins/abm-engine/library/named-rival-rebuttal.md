# Named-rival rebuttal

- **Archetype**: a named exec/company makes a public claim; the author counters it with their own company's data
- **Funnel stage**: BOFU
- **ABM mechanic**: picks a respectful public fight with a named account — their org, your org, and everyone with an opinion piles in. The counter-data glorifies your own company while keeping the named account in every reshare.
- **Platform**: LinkedIn
- **Source examples**: (refreshed by the scheduled library-refresh task; pinned entries never auto-replaced)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anniedeanzaitzeff_this-past-weekend-nikes-ceo-shared-that-activity-7186039378182295553-cXaS — Annie Dean, 8,063 reactions / 281 comments, Apr 2024 (Atlassian data vs Nike CEO)
  - https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anniedeanzaitzeff_return-to-office-mandates-arent-the-solution-activity-7241859008456716288-jyYP — Annie Dean, 11,875 reactions / 592 comments, Sep 2024 (vs Amazon RTO memo, citing Airbnb's Chesky)
- **Hook pattern**: "This past weekend {company}'s CEO shared that {claim}." / "{policy} isn't the solution to the real problem: {reframe}."
- **Skeleton**:
  1. Hook restating the named claim neutrally (date-stamped)
  2. "At {our company} we're seeing the opposite:" — the counter-signal
  3. The data: study scope, n, what was measured, what it found (2–4 lines)
  4. Reframe: what the claim gets wrong about the underlying problem
  5. Principle close ("this moment demands more") — no gloating
- **Rhythm & formatting**: 150–300 words; asterisk or italic emphasis on the pivot word; measured, senior tone; zero snark.
- **Why it works**: conflict is the algorithm's favorite food; the named claim supplies reach, the data supplies credibility, the restraint supplies seniority.
- **Adaptation notes**: the claim must be public, recent, and from an org your ICP watches. Counter-data must be YOUR real data (context Proof section only).
- **Watch-outs**: HIGH RISK — punch up only (bigger company, public statement); never name-call the exec; if your data is thin the whole post collapses; run within days of the claim.

## Top example — full post text
Source: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/anniedeanzaitzeff_this-past-weekend-nikes-ceo-shared-that-activity-7186039378182295553-cXaS (Annie Dean, 8,063 reactions)

```
This past weekend Nike's CEO shared that remote work was hurting innovation.

At Atlassian we're seeing signals that distributed teams are * more * innovative.

We analyzed data from 4,000 shipit teams — shipit is our 2x annual internal hackathon — across 8 different shipit hackathons since 2021. We found that distributed (vs local) shipit teams were more diverse (range of skills, members spread across more crafts, more levels) and that they were more likely to produce a demo and receive a higher rank by voters.

When we commit to doing it well, organizing our work online-first (vs office-first) is often * better *. "We're struggling to innovate" is an admission that a company isn't evolving how work gets done, not evidence that the office drives innovation.
```
