# Worked examples (all six message types)

Use these as the model. Each shows the register and, where useful, the **bad version → fixed version** so the tell is obvious. The soft register is the default; the sharp register is only for low-warmth targets who reward a take.

> These use sample/illustrative profiles and a placeholder client to demonstrate technique. Swap in your own targets and clients.

---

## 1. Connection request note (≤300 chars)

| Trigger | Note | Why |
|---|---|---|
| They liked the post | "saw you liked the post, figured we should connect." | Trigger + stop. No question, no pitch. |
| They commented | "saw your comment on the contact-center thread, figured we should connect." | Anchors to the specific comment, not "your comment." |
| They viewed the profile | "saw you in the stalkers, figured we should connect." | Playful, peer-level, zero effort to accept. |
| Cold, no trigger | "mobile accept at PNC was a clean ship. figured i'd reach out." | No trigger to anchor, so lead with the non-obvious compliment. Drop "figured we should connect." |

**Bad → fixed:**
- ❌ "Thanks in advance for connecting — I'd love to learn more about what you're building." (thanks opener + pitch + permission)
- ✅ "saw your post on duty drawback, figured we should connect."

## 2. First DM after they accept (the core type)

**Soft register — the default.** Use these as the model.

| Target (sample) | Final pick | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Product/CPTO → CEO of an industrial-tech company; connect accepted; (sample client) | "saw you just took over as ceo of [company], super impressive. must have been wild jumping from the product side to running the whole thing. what was that like?" | Names the real, non-obvious thing (product → CEO). Warm compliment. Question is dead easy over text. No industry take, no assumptions. Lowercase, reads like a text. |
| Same target (alt — homework flex) | "was looking through your profile and saw you went from interim cto of a dog-walking app to ceo of a [parent-co] company. love that. how'd you pull that off?" | Proves a deep read without turning it into an AI observation. "love that, how'd you pull that off" keeps it light and human. |
| Same target (alt — warm live wire) | "saw the take-your-child-to-work-day post, nearly 100 kids in the building. love that. is that a yearly thing for you guys?" | A human, non-business live wire that's almost impossible not to answer. Stands out in a CEO's inbox precisely because it isn't about work. |

**Sharper register — only for sharp/low-warmth targets who reward a take.**

| Target (sample) | Final pick | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 8-yr bank exec → head of a function at a new bank; connect accepted | "Impressive stint at [old co]. What pulled you over to [new co]? Got bored of getting promoted?" | "Impressive stint," not "eight years." Peer-level. Second question is humor that flatters the first. |
| Banker → product, 670% of goal at prior co; connect accepted | "670% of goal at [co] is wild. What got you into product after putting up that kind of run?" | Names a specific outlier number (homework flex). "Is wild" is texting voice. Question rides the compliment. |

**Bad → fixed (the most common miss):**
- ❌ "Going from interim CTO to CEO of a [parent-co] company is a jump most people never make — you're clearly built for the operator seat." (banned "[X] to [Y] is a jump most people never make" + "seat")
- ✅ "must have been crazy going from interim cto to ceo of a [parent-co] company. what was that like?"

## 3. Follow-up when they go cold

| Situation | Follow-up | Why |
|---|---|---|
| No reply, they posted a new hire since | "saw you just added a head of CX. curious how you're thinking about the build vs. buy on support." | New live wire, shifted angle, not a repeat of the first question. |
| No reply, company in the news | "saw the funding news, congrats. changes the picture a bit, no?" | Short, fresh hook, easy yes/no entry point. |
| No reply, they reposted something | "that repost on agent deflection rates — is that the number your team actually watches?" | Pulls a real signal off their feed, one soft question. |

**Bad → fixed:**
- ❌ "Just bumping this up in case it got buried — did you see my note?" (nag + permission-seeking)
- ✅ "saw your post on the new contact-center stack. curious what you make of it."

## 4. Reply when they engage but don't commit

The first message earned the reply; now you've earned a sharper question. **Mirror their length and energy.**

| They sent | You send | Why |
|---|---|---|
| One line: "ha, it was a wild ride honestly." | "i bet. what surprised you most going from building product to owning the whole number?" | One line back, slightly deeper question now that they've opened the door. |
| A real take: "support's moving to agents faster than people admit." | "yeah, that tracks. the part i'm watching is how fast deflection actually holds up in production. what are you seeing?" | Mirror the take, add one of your own, return a question. This is where an industry angle is finally allowed. |
| A question back: "what are you working on these days?" | answer it in one honest line, then return a question about them — don't dump a pitch. | Keeps it peer-to-peer, not a sales handoff. |

**Bad → fixed:**
- ❌ They send a one-liner and you reply with five sentences and a calendar link. (over-escalation)
- ✅ Match the one-liner with a one-liner + one question.

## 5. Call ask (only after a real back-and-forth)

| Context | Ask | Why |
|---|---|---|
| They've traded 2-3 messages on agent deflection | "got 15 min next week? want to hear how you're thinking about deflection holding up at scale." | Their expertise to extract, framed as value to *them*. Specific to what they already engaged on. |
| They've shared a strong opinion on tariffs | "this is the stuff i could talk about all day. got 15 next week to keep going?" | Light, peer-level, no agenda stated. |

**Bad → fixed:**
- ❌ "Would love to hop on a quick call to learn more about your business and see if there's a fit." (vendor language + permission + "learn about your business")
- ✅ "got 15 min next week? want to hear how you're reading the drawback changes."

## 6. Rejection comeback / cold shoulder (rare, ~1%)

Only after a real attempt. Stay amused, never aggrieved. Humor signals the sender isn't invested, which keeps the door open.

| Situation | Comeback | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Ghosted after one real exchange | "all good, looking forward to seeing you in the feed." | Warm, unbothered, leaves the door cracked. |
| Soft "not interested right now" | "fair enough, see you in the algorithm." | Light, no pushback, no desperation. |
| Hard ignore | "all good, i guess you can be another number in my network." | Slightly cheeky, signals zero neediness. |

**Bad → fixed:**
- ❌ "I understand you're busy, but I really think this could be valuable — open to reconsidering?" (aggrieved + pushy + needy)
- ✅ "all good, looking forward to seeing you in the feed."

## Thin-profile example

Target is a VP with a headline, two logos, and no posts. Nothing to flex on.

- ❌ Don't invent: "saw you led the platform rebuild at [company]" when no rebuild is mentioned anywhere.
- ✅ Anchor to what's real: "saw you moved over to lead [function] at [company], nice. how's it been so far?"
- ✅ Or fall back to the trigger-only connection note and say: "thin profile, nothing real to anchor a first DM — got a recent post or a mutual? otherwise I'd just send the connect note."
