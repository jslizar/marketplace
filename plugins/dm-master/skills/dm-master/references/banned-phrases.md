# Banned phrases (hard rules)

These came from real feedback. No exceptions. Run every draft against this list before delivering. Most entries include the **flip** — what to do instead.

## Words

- **Em dashes.** Absolute. Use periods or commas.
- **"Real"** as a compliment intensifier ("real ship," "real move," "real seat," "real operator arc," "real P&L seat").
- **"Seat"** as a noun for a role ("the seat I'd want," "real operator seat").

## Phrases

- "Thanks for connecting" / "Thanks for the connect" / any thanks opener → open with "saw you [did the thing]" instead.
- "The kind of [move/seat/bet] I respect."
- "The [move/bet/seat] I'd make too."
- "Smart arc."
- "Sharp pivot."
- "Big jump" as a standalone compliment → use "big move."
- "Humans plus AI" framing.
- "Lives or dies on [X]" as a cold opener (a take dropped from nowhere).
- **"most people only read about" / "most people only ever X" / any "most people [X]" framing** — textbook AI, instant tell.
- **"is a rare combination" / "a rare combo"** / naming the impressive thing as a thing.
- "Most people I talk to," "Most leaders in this space," anything starting with "Most..." — AI tell.

## Move patterns (the structural tells)

- **"[X] to [Y] is a [jump/move/combination] most [people] never make."** The single most common AI tell. Any sentence that names an accomplishment then editorializes on how rare/impressive it is in the abstract.
  - **Flip:** "must have been crazy going from [X] to [Y]. what was that like?"

- **Naming the impressive thing as a noun-phrase observation** ("a product builder now owning the full P&L," "a hands-on COO who still runs the DMS"). Don't observe it at them.
  - **Flip:** ask about the *experience* of it. "must have been wild taking on the whole P&L. how's that been?"

- **Industry takes or claims in a first DM.** Telling someone what their world is like, what's pressuring their business, or how their market works before they've said a word back. Reads as a pitch.
  - **Flip:** hold it for the reply. The first message earns the reply; the reply earns the take.

- **Deep / layered / comparison questions in a first DM** ("how are you reading X differently than a lifer would," "what's surprised you most about the cost side," anything asking them to diagnose or compare).
  - **Flip:** one soft question answerable in a single thumb-typed line. Save the sharp one for after they reply.

- **Resume narration** ("Eight years at PNC, four VP titles") → "long stint" / "long run."

- **Specific resume numbers as the compliment** ("eight years," "four titles," "twenty-five-year run") → "long stint." (Exception: a genuine *outlier* number used as the hook, e.g. "670% of goal is wild," is fine.)

- **Capitalized job-function phrases as nouns** ("Retail Transformation into Merchant Services is a sharp pivot") → read as LinkedIn, not text. Lowercase it and make it a question.

- **Abstract questions** ("What's the biggest blind spot," "What no one else is talking about," "What keeps you up at night") → too hard to answer in one line.

- **Asking permission** ("Mind if I ask," "Hope you don't mind me reaching out").

- **Multi-part questions** stacked into one message → ask one.

- **Long takes before the question** (more than one short sentence of setup).

- **Question without an ego stroke first** in a first-DM context.

- **Title-case / sentence-case stiffness** when a lowercase text would read warmer → mix in lowercase.

## Fast self-check

If a draft contains any of: an em dash, the word "seat" or "real," a "most people" / "rare combination" phrase, an "[X] to [Y] is a [move]" structure, an industry claim, or a question that takes more than one line to answer — rewrite it before delivering.
